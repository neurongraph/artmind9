## Purpose

Establish standardized procedures for closing customer accounts (SmartSaver, Current, Mortgage) through customer-initiated requests or bank-initiated closures due to policy violations, regulatory concerns, or fraud.

---

## Scope

Applies to:
- Customer-initiated account closures
- Bank-initiated closures (regulatory, fraud, policy violation)
- All product types (savings, current, mortgages)
- All channels (branch, online, phone, mail)

---

## Process Overview

```
Closure Request Received
    ↓
Verify Customer Identity
    ↓
Calculate Final Balance
    ↓
Process Standing Orders/Direct Debits
    ↓
Closure Type Assessment (Customer vs. Bank-Initiated)
    ↓
    ├─ Customer-Initiated: Process normally
    └─ Bank-Initiated: Investigate, document reason
    ↓
Transfer/Remit Final Balance
    ↓
Issue Closure Confirmation
    ↓
Archive Records (6 years)
    ↓
Account Marked "Closed" in System
```

**Timeline:** 3–5 business days (customer-initiated), Immediate (bank-initiated with notice)

---

## Step 1: Closure Request Reception

### Customer-Initiated Closure

**Request Channels:**
- **Branch:** In-person request to manager
- **Phone:** Call customer service (0800-555-2265)
- **Online:** Submit closure form in account portal
- **Mail:** Written request to head office

**Information Required:**
- Account number
- Full name (matching account)
- Address (for verification)
- Reason for closure (optional, for feedback)
- Preferred method of final payment (check, bank transfer, cash)

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Record request (date, time, method, customer contact info)
2. Verify customer identity (security questions or document verification)
3. Confirm no holds on account (freezes, disputes, fraud investigation)
4. Explain closure implications (no further transactions, interest stops, records archived)

### Bank-Initiated Closure

**Triggers for Bank-Initiated Closure:**
- 🚨 **Regulatory Reason:** Compliance violation, AML breach, regulatory order
- 🚨 **Fraud:** Account used fraudulently, customer identity issue, unauthorized access
- 🚨 **Policy Violation:** Breach of terms & conditions, customer misconduct
- 🚨 **Dormancy:** No activity + failed contact attempts (after 5 years, may close)

**Process:**
1. Manager/Compliance Head initiates closure
2. Document reason in system (regulatory, fraud, policy)
3. Prepare notice to customer (30-day notice required, except emergencies)
4. Customer notification sent (letter explaining reason)

**Emergency Bank-Initiated Closure (No Notice):**
- Immediate account freeze (if fraud/security risk confirmed)
- Closure follows within 7 days
- Reason documented (regulatory/fraud/security)
- Customer notified same day

---

## Step 2: Account Verification

**Verification Checks:**
- ✅ Account exists and is active (not already closed)
- ✅ Account ownership confirmed (match customer identity)
- ✅ No active disputes (complaint, chargeback, reversal)
- ✅ No legal holds (litigation, regulatory investigation, tax lien)
- ✅ No outstanding fraud investigation

**If Issues Found:**
- Escalate to manager/Compliance Head
- Resolve issue before proceeding
- Document delay reason in system

**Typical Resolution Time:** Same day

---

## Step 3: Final Balance Calculation

### Balance Calculation Process

**Step 1: Collect All Transactions**
- Pull all transactions since last statement
- Include pending transactions (standing orders, direct debits due)
- Verify no unprocessed deposits/withdrawals

**Step 2: Calculate Final Balance**
- Ledger balance (posted transactions)
- Add any pending interest (if accrual period not yet completed)
- Subtract any pending fees (overdraft, if applicable)
- Verify balance matches account record in AMS

**Step 3: Document Balance**
- Print final statement
- Include calculation date
- List all pending transactions
- Final balance clearly stated

**Step 4: Approval**
- Operations manager verifies calculation
- Sign-off on final statement
- File in account closure record

### Example Calculation

```
SmartSaver Account Closure Example:

Ledger Balance (as of closure date):     £4,250.32
Pending Interest (current month):        +£14.58
Pending Fees (none):                     £0.00
───────────────────────────────────────────────
FINAL BALANCE TO REMIT:                  £4,264.90
```

---

## Step 4: Process Standing Orders & Direct Debits

### Standing Order Cancellation

**Actions:**
- Cancel all future standing orders (effective immediately)
- Last standing order executes if scheduled before closure date
- Customer notified of cancellation
- Beneficiary(ies) may receive cancellation notice (optional)

**Timeline:** Same day as closure request

### Direct Debit Cancellation

**Actions:**
- Cancel all Direct Debit mandates (effective immediately)
- Notify billers of cancellation (bank's responsibility)
- Customer advised to confirm cancellation with billers separately
- No further DD transactions can be processed

**Timeline:** Same day as closure request

**Important:** Customer responsible for ensuring billers received cancellation notice

---

## Step 5: Closure Type Assessment

### Customer-Initiated Closure (Standard Process)

**Characteristics:**
- Customer voluntarily requests closure
- No regulatory/fraud concerns
- Account in good standing
- Process proceeds as planned

**Actions:**
- Proceed to Step 6 (final payment)
- No special documentation needed
- Standard closure confirmation sent

**Timeline:** 3–5 business days

### Bank-Initiated Closure (Special Process)

**Characteristics:**
- Bank initiates closure (regulatory, fraud, policy)
- May be urgent or emergency scenario
- Account may be frozen

**Actions:**
- Document closure reason in system
- Compliance/manager approval required
- Legal review (if significant financial impact)
- Customer notice (30 days, or immediate if emergency)
- Investigation documentation attached

**Timeline:** Immediate (freeze) + 7–30 days (notice period)

---

## Step 6: Final Balance Payment

### Payment Method Options

**1. Bank Transfer (Preferred)**
- Customer provides receiving bank details
- Transfer initiated immediately (if funds available)
- Timeline: 1–2 business days (FPS)
- Confirmation sent to customer

**2. Check**
- Check issued in customer name
- Mailed to registered address
- Timeline: 3–5 business days
- Tracking number provided

**3. Cash (Branch Only)**
- Customer collects at branch
- Teller issues cash receipt
- Immediate payment
- Limited to £5,000 (regulatory requirement for cash)
- Larger amounts: Check or transfer recommended

**4. Hold for Customer Instruction**
- Balance held in suspense account (maximum 6 months)
- Customer instructed to provide payment details
- Reminder sent at 3 months
- Transferred to unclaimed money fund after 6 months

### Payment Processing

**Steps:**
1. Confirm payment method with customer
2. Obtain bank details (if transfer) or address (if check)
3. Process payment within 5 business days
4. Confirm payment sent to customer
5. Record payment in system (closure complete)

---

## Step 7: Account Status Change

### System Updates

**Changes Made:**
- Account status: "Active" → "Closed"
- Closure date recorded
- Closure reason documented
- Final balance recorded
- All standing orders/DDs marked "Cancelled"

**Links Updated:**
- Customer profile: Account marked as closed
- Product statistics: Removed from active account count
- Fraud detection: Account excluded from monitoring
- Interest calculation: Stops at closure date

**Access Restrictions:**
- No new transactions permitted
- Online banking access: Disabled
- Card (if issued): Automatically deactivated
- Standing orders/DDs: Blocked

**Timeline:** Same day as final payment

---

## Step 8: Customer Notification

### Closure Confirmation Letter

**Contents:**
- Account number and product name
- Closure date
- Final balance
- Payment method and timeline
- Standing order/DD cancellation confirmation
- Records retention notice (6 years archive)
- Contact information (questions)

**Delivery:**
- Email (if email on file)
- Postal mail (required, to registered address)
- Sent within 2 business days of closure

**Tone:** Professional, thanking customer for business

---

## Step 9: Record Retention & Archive

### Document Archival

**Records to Archive (6 Years):**
- Account opening documents (KYC, ID, address verification)
- All transaction records (statements, transaction log)
- Account closure request and reason
- Final statement and balance confirmation
- Payment proof (receipt, transfer confirmation, check cleared)
- Any dispute or complaint records

**Storage Location:**
- Secure document management system (DMS)
- Encrypted, access-controlled
- Audit trail of all access

**Retention Period:** 6 years from closure date (per POCA, AML Regulations)

**Destruction Procedure:**
- At 6-year mark: Schedule for secure destruction
- Shred paper documents
- Securely delete digital records
- Certificate of destruction obtained

## Closure Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Standard Customer-Initiated Closure

```
Customer: Closes SmartSaver Account

Day 1:
- Customer calls 0800-555-2265
- Requests closure, all balance to bank transfer
- Provides receiving bank details
- System notes closure request

Day 2:
- Operations processes request
- Final balance calculated: £4,264.90
- Payment initiated (FPS transfer)
- Closure confirmation letter generated

Day 3:
- Customer receives bank transfer
- Receives confirmation letter
- Account status changed to "Closed"
- Records archived

Result: ✅ Closed in 3 days, customer satisfied
```

### Scenario 2: Bank-Initiated (Policy Violation)

```
Customer: Suspected unauthorized use, investigation underway

Day 1:
- Account frozen immediately (fraud hold)
- Investigation initiated by Financial Crime team
- Customer notified of freeze (no reason disclosed—tipping off risk)

Days 2–7:
- Investigation completed
- Unauthorized transactions confirmed
- Refund processed to customer
- Account closure decision made

Day 8:
- Notice sent to customer: "Account closure due to policy violation"
- 30-day notice period begins

Day 38:
- 30 days passed
- Final balance transferred (minus any chargebacks)
- Account officially closed
- Records archived

Result: ✅ Closed after 30-day notice, customer refunded
```

## Quality Assurance

### Closure Audit Checklist

**Before Marking "Closed":**
- [ ] Closure request properly documented
- [ ] Customer identity verified
- [ ] Final balance calculated correctly
- [ ] All standing orders/DDs cancelled
- [ ] Final payment initiated
- [ ] Closure confirmation sent to customer
- [ ] Account status updated in system
- [ ] Records flagged for 6-year archival

**Monthly Audit:**
- 5+ closure files reviewed
- Check completeness and accuracy
- Verify timelines met
- Assess for compliance issues

## Common Issues & Resolution

| Issue | Cause | Resolution |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Customer requests re-opening | Changed mind | New account opening required (full KYC) |
| Pending standing order on closure date | Timing issue | Execute final SO, then close |
| Dispute received after closure | Customer claim | Reopen investigation, may reverse closure if warranted |
| Customer doesn't collect check | Payment delay | Hold 6 months, escalate to unclaimed money procedures |
