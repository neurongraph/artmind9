## Executive Summary

FirstUK Bank retains customer and operational documents for periods mandated by law and business need, then destroys records securely to minimize data breach risk and compliance burden.

---

## Purpose & Scope

### Purpose

To establish retention schedules that:
- Comply with legal and regulatory obligations
- Support dispute resolution and audits
- Minimize data retention risk (GDPR: data minimization)
- Ensure secure destruction
- Maintain audit trail

### Scope

Applies to:
- All customer documents
- All transaction records
- All policy/procedure documentation
- All audit and compliance files
- All physical and digital records

---

## Policy Statement

**FirstUK Bank retains documents only as long as legally required or business-justified, then destroys them securely.**

## Extended Retention

**Retention Extended If:**
- Customer dispute is active (until resolution + 6 years)
- Regulatory investigation ongoing (until conclusion)
- Legal action pending (until conclusion + 6 years)
- Customer requests retention (must be documented)
- Regulatory requirement (GDPR, POCA, etc.)

**Process:**
1. Document placed on legal hold
2. Marked "Do Not Destroy"
3. Reassess annually
4. Destroy when hold lifted

---

## Early Destruction

**Some documents destroyed early if:**

**Email (non-compliance):**
- Routine operational emails: 1 year
- Archived to external storage: 3 years
- Compliance emails: 7 years (legal hold)

**System Logs:**
- General transaction logs: 1 year
- Archived for backup: 30 days
- Compliance/audit logs: 1 year minimum

**Process:**
1. Categorize document
2. Confirm no legal hold
3. Schedule for destruction
4. Destroy securely
5. Confirm and document

---

## Destruction Methods

### Physical Documents

**Shredding:**
- On-site shredder (if <10 pages/day)
- Shred before recycle
- Document destruction log

**Professional Shredding:**
- Third-party secure destruction company
- Certificate of destruction provided
- Scheduled pickup (monthly/quarterly)
- Chain of custody maintained

### Digital Records

**Secure Deletion:**
- Data overwriting (DoD standard, 3-pass)
- Encrypted deletion (AES-256)
- Physical destruction (hard drives)

**Process:**
1. Identify files to destroy
2. Quarantine to destruction folder
3. Secure delete (not just recycle bin)
4. Verify deletion
5. Certificate generated

- Audit trail maintained

## Customer Requests

**Customer Right to Data Deletion:**

**GDPR Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"):**
- Customer can request deletion
- FirstUK Bank may refuse if legal obligation exists
- Exception: POCA, AML records (cannot delete if regulatory required)

**Procedure:**
1. Customer requests deletion (in writing)
2. FirstUK Bank assesses legal hold
3. If no hold: Destroy and confirm
4. If hold: Explain reason and timeframe

---

## Dispute & Legal Holds

**Litigation Hold:**
- Customer dispute or legal action
- Retain all relevant documents
- Document marked "Legal Hold"
- Cannot destroy during dispute
- Destroy 6 years after resolution

**Regulatory Investigation:**
- Regulator requests hold
- Retain all specified documents
- Follow regulator timeline
- Destroy per regulator instruction

## Audit & Compliance

**Records Audit:**
- Annual audit of retention practices
- Sample file review (destruction logs)
- Compliance with retention schedule
- Unauthorized destruction assessment

**Regulatory Audit:**
- FCA/PRA periodic reviews
- Document retention sampling
- POCA compliance assessment
