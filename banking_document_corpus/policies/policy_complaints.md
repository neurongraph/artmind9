## Executive Summary

FirstUK Bank is committed to handling customer complaints fairly, efficiently, and in accordance with FCA requirements. This policy establishes procedures for complaint receipt, investigation, resolution, and regulatory reporting.

---

## Purpose & Scope

### Purpose

To establish complaint handling procedures that:
- Acknowledge all complaints promptly
- Investigate issues thoroughly
- Resolve complaints fairly
- Report to FCA as required
- Prevent repeat issues
- Maintain customer relationship

### Scope

Applies to:
- All customer complaints (product, service, policy)
- All channels (branch, phone, email, online)
- All staff
- All accounts and products

---

## Policy Statement

**FirstUK Bank treats all customer complaints seriously and resolves them fairly within regulatory timeframes.**

---

## Complaint Definition

**Complaint:** Any expression of dissatisfaction by customer about FirstUK Bank's service or product.

### Examples

**Service Complaints:**
- Long processing time
- Incorrect information provided
- Poor customer service
- System error/outage impact

**Product Complaints:**
- Wrong interest rate applied
- Fee incorrectly charged
- Terms & conditions misunderstood
- Feature not working

**Financial Loss:**
- Unauthorized transaction
- Fraudulent activity
- Incorrect balance
- Fees wrongly charged

## Complaint Receipt & Logging

### How to Complain

**Channels:**
- Branch (in person, immediate log)
- Phone (0800-555-2265, recorded and logged)
- Email (support@firstuk.bank, immediate log)
- Online (customer portal complaint form)
- Letter (Complaints, FirstUK Bank, 125 Threadneedle Street, London)

### Immediate Actions

1. **Record Complaint**
   - Date and time received
   - Customer name and account number
   - Complaint details (verbatim if verbal)
   - Channel received through
   - Staff member receiving

2. **Acknowledge to Customer**
   - Branch: Immediate verbal acknowledgment + written confirmation
   - Phone: Verbal acknowledgment + written confirmation within 24 hours
   - Email: Automated receipt confirmation
   - Letter: Written acknowledgment within 2 business days

3. **Log in System**
   - Create complaint record in CRM
   - Assign complaint ID
   - Tag by category
   - Assign to appropriate handler

## Complaint Handling Process

### Step 1: Initial Assessment (Day 1–2)

**Triage by Severity:**

**Urgent (Immediate Action):**
- Financial loss >£1,000
- Fraud/security breach
- Service outage impact
- Regulatory concern
→ **Escalate to Manager immediately**

**Standard:**
- Regular service issues
- Fee disputes <£1,000
- Operational delays
→ **Process normally**

### Step 2: Investigation (Days 2–5)

**Information Gathering:**
- Review account records
- Check transaction history
- Review relevant procedures/policies
- Interview involved staff (if applicable)
- Assess policy compliance

**Documentation:**
- Record all findings
- Note policy/procedure applicable
- Determine if FirstUK Bank at fault
- Quantify financial loss (if any)

### Step 3: Resolution (Days 5–8)

**Possible Outcomes:**

**If FirstUK Bank at Fault:**
- Full financial compensation for loss
- Plus interest (8% per annum) if appropriate
- Plus additional compensation if distress caused
- Written apology
- Process improvement (to prevent recurrence)

**If Customer at Fault:**
- Explanation of policy/procedure
- Evidence provided
- Polite decline of compensation
- Educational tone (help customer understand)

**If Unclear/Inconclusive:**
- Explain evidence reviewed
- Acknowledge customer perspective
- Offer partial compensation (if appropriate)
- Explain further steps (e.g., external ombudsman)

### Step 4: Response (Day 8 Latest)

**Written Response Must Include:**
- Acknowledgment of complaint
- Summary of investigation
- Clear decision and reasoning
- Compensation offered (if applicable)
- Explanation of right to escalate

**Delivery:**
- Email or letter (customer choice)
- Plain language (avoid jargon)
- Tone: Professional, respectful

## Response Timeframes (FCA Requirements)

**Maximum Response Times:**

| Complaint Type | Timeframe | Escalation |
|---|---|---|
| Simple (resolve quickly) | 1 business day | Manager if escalated |
| Standard | 8 calendar days | Director if unresolved |
| Complex | 8 weeks (FCA extension) | FCA notification if exceeded |

**8-Week Clock:**
- Starts: When complaint received
- Stops: When response sent to customer
- Can be extended 8 weeks if: Complex investigation justified, customer agrees

## Complaint Categories & Handling

### 1. Service Complaints

**Example:** Long wait, incorrect information, rude staff

**Investigation:**
- Check timeline
- Review procedures followed
- Assess reasonableness
- Interview staff if needed

**Resolution:**
- If at fault: Apology + compensation for inconvenience
- Compensation: Up to £100 for minor inconvenience, more if significant distress/loss
- Learning: Process improvement to prevent recurrence

### 2. Fee Disputes

**Example:** Unexpected charge, overdraft fee, card replacement fee

**Investigation:**
- Review terms & conditions
- Check if fee applied correctly
- Assess if fee disclosed clearly
- Review customer history

**Resolution:**
- If fee applied correctly per T&Cs: Explain and decline reversal
- If fee error: Reverse charge + interest
- If unclear T&Cs: Refund + apology

### 3. Account Balance Issues

**Example:** Missing credit, wrong balance shown, interest miscalculated

**Investigation:**
- Reconcile transactions
- Check system records
- Verify calculations
- Review any hold periods

**Resolution:**
- If bank error: Correct balance + interest on missing amount
- If customer misunderstanding: Explain (educate, don't penalize)
- If system error: Urgent IT escalation to fix

### 4. Fraud/Unauthorized Transactions

**Example:** Customer claims fraudulent transaction

**Investigation:**
- Obtain customer statement
- Review transaction details
- Check if customer authorized
- Assess fraud likelihood
- Fraud team investigation (if needed)

**Resolution:**
- If fraud confirmed: Full reimbursement within 10 business days + apology
- If customer liable: Explain (educational)
- If disputed: Further investigation + FCA guidance

**See:** [[policy_fraud]] for fraud-specific procedures

### 5. Unauthorized Account Access (Account Takeover)

**Example:** Customer discovers account used without permission

**Investigation:**
- Immediate account freeze
- Transaction review
- Authentication review (password change?)
- Fraud team escalation
- Law enforcement notification (if warranted)

**Resolution:**
- Full reimbursement of fraudulent transactions
- Account security reset
- Replacement card/online access reset
- Credit monitoring (if identity theft)
- Regular follow-up

## Escalation Matrix

**Complaint Escalation by Severity & Involvement:**

| Level | Authority | Timeline | Complaints |
|-------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Frontline** | Customer Service Agent | <1 day | Simple, low-value |
| **Manager** | Complaints Manager | <5 days | Moderate issues, <£500 loss |
| **Director** | Head of Customer Service | <8 days | Significant issues, >£500 loss |
| **Escalated** | CEO/Board | 8+ weeks | Major failures, reputational risk |

**Escalation Triggers:**
- Complaint unresolved after manager review
- Financial compensation >£500
- Regulatory or reputational concern
- Media/legal involvement
- Customer escalates to FCA

## Compensation Framework

**Financial Compensation Guidelines:**

| Scenario | Compensation Range | Basis |
|----------|-------------------|-------|
| Service delay (1–2 days) | £0–£50 | Inconvenience |
| Service delay (3–5 days) | £50–£100 | Operational disruption |
| Service failure (>5 days) | £100–£300 | Significant impact |
| Financial loss (error) | Full loss + 8% interest | Actual loss + interest |
| Serious distress | £250–£500+ | Emotional impact |

**Limits:**
- <£100: Customer Service Manager approval
- £100–£500: Director approval
- >£500: CEO approval
- >£1,000: Board notification + CEO approval

## FCA Reporting

### Reportable Complaints

**Must Report to FCA:**
- Complaints alleging breaches of FCA rules
- Complaints about financial services
- Complaints about conduct
- Complaints causing financial loss
- Systemic issues

### FCA Reporting Requirement (COBS)

**Who Files:** FirstUK Bank (directly to FCA)  
**When:** As data to FCA, not automatically disclosed  
**What:** Aggregated complaint statistics (quarterly)  
**Disclosure:** Customers can escalate to FCA if unresolved after 8 weeks

### FCA Escalation (Customer-Initiated)

**If Customer Unhappy:**
- FirstUK Bank provides FCA contact information
- Customer can escalate to Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS)
- FOS is independent, free to customer
- FOS decision is binding

**Escalation Letter Must Include:**
- Statement of rights
- FOS contact: 0800 023 4567, www.financialombudsman.org.uk
- Time limit: Customer must escalate within 6 months of final response

## Learning & Improvement

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- Identify systemic complaints (same issue, multiple customers)
- Analyze root cause
- Implement corrective action
- Monitor effectiveness

**Examples:**
- High "slow processing" complaints → Review SOP, add resources
- Multiple fee disputes → Improve T&C clarity, better disclosure
- Fraud complaints → Strengthen security measures

## Staff Responsibilities

### All Staff

- Take all complaints seriously
- Record accurately and promptly
- Escalate appropriately
- Follow procedures
- Maintain confidentiality
- Treat customers respectfully

### Customer Service/Branch Staff

- Receive complaints
- Provide immediate acknowledgment
- Log in system
- Escalate urgent issues

### Complaints Handlers

- Investigate thoroughly
- Document findings
- Follow resolution procedures
- Communicate clearly with customer
- Meet timeframes
- Escalate if needed

### Management

- Approve complaints >£500
- Monitor trends
- Implement corrective actions
- Report to Board
- External regulatory liaison
