## Executive Summary

FirstUK Bank maintains an Operational Risk Policy to identify, assess, and mitigate operational risks (process failures, systems outages, fraud, compliance breaches, etc.) that could impact customers, staff, or business continuity.

---

## Purpose & Scope

### Purpose

To establish procedures for operational risk management that:
- Identify operational risks proactively
- Assess risk impact and likelihood
- Implement mitigation controls
- Monitor risk metrics
- Report to senior management and Board

### Scope

Applies to:
- All business processes
- All systems and technology
- All staff and contractors
- All products and services
- All geographic locations

---

## Policy Statement

**FirstUK Bank operates controls to prevent, detect, and mitigate operational risks, with particular focus on process failures, system outages, fraud, compliance breaches, and external events.**

---

## Operational Risk Categories

**Operational Risk = Risk of loss due to inadequate/failed processes, people, systems, or external events.**

### 1. Process Risk

**Process failures causing errors or delays:**
- Account opening errors
- Payment processing failures
- Reconciliation errors
- Fraud detection failures

**Mitigation:**
- Standard operating procedures (SOPs)
- Staff training
- Supervisory review
- Automation where possible

### 2. System Risk

**Technology failures causing service disruption:**
- System outages
- Data corruption
- Integration failures
- Security breaches

**Mitigation:**
- System redundancy/backup
- Disaster recovery planning
- Change management
- Security controls

### 3. People Risk

**Staff errors or misconduct:**
- Unauthorized transactions
- Policy violations
- Negligence
- Theft/fraud

**Mitigation:**
- Training and awareness
- Segregation of duties
- Access controls
- Monitoring and audits

### 4. Compliance Risk

**Regulatory violations or breaches:**
- KYC failures
- AML violations
- GDPR breaches
- Reporting errors

**Mitigation:**
- Policies and procedures
- Compliance training
- Regular audits
- Regulatory monitoring

### 5. Fraud Risk

**Internal or external fraud:**
- Customer fraud (unauthorized transactions)
- Employee fraud (embezzlement)
- Third-party fraud (vendor fraud)
- System fraud (data manipulation)

**Mitigation:**
- Fraud detection systems
- Segregation of duties
- Whistleblower procedures
- Investigation protocols

### 6. Reputational Risk

**Reputational damage from failures:**
- Customer complaints (social media)
- Regulatory violations (press)
- System outages (service disruption)
- Fraud/misconduct (scandal)

**Mitigation:**
- Service quality focus
- Regulatory compliance
- Incident response
- Media management

### 7. External Risk

**External events beyond control:**
- Natural disasters
- Cyberattacks
- Vendor failures
- Regulatory changes

**Mitigation:**
- Business continuity planning
- Cybersecurity measures
- Vendor management
- Regulatory monitoring

## Risk Assessment Framework

### Risk Identification

**Continuous Process:**
- Annual risk assessment (by department)
- Process walk-throughs with management
- Incident trend analysis
- Regulatory change assessment
- External threat monitoring

**Risk Register:**
- Centralized log of identified risks
- Risk ID, description, impact, likelihood
- Current and residual risk rating
- Responsible party

### Risk Scoring

**Impact Scale (1–5):**

| Score | Impact | Loss Amount | Examples |
|-------|--------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Minimal | <£10k | Minor delay, low customer impact |
| 2 | Low | £10k–£50k | Service disruption <1 hour, few customers |
| 3 | Medium | £50k–£250k | System outage 1–4 hours, many customers |
| 4 | High | £250k–£1M | Regulatory breach, significant loss, reputational |
| 5 | Critical | >£1M | Major breach, large loss, license risk |

**Likelihood Scale (1–5):**

| Score | Likelihood | Frequency | Examples |
|-------|------------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | Rare | <1 in 10 years | Asteroid impact (theoretically) |
| 2 | Unlikely | 1 in 3–10 years | Major system failure, unexpected event |
| 3 | Possible | 1 per year | Process error, occasional system glitch |
| 4 | Likely | Multiple per year | Fraud detection, customer complaint |
| 5 | Very Likely | Monthly+ | Daily transaction errors, system slowness |

**Risk Rating Matrix:**

```
Impact    1    2    3    4    5
Likelihood
5 (VH)    5    10   15   20   25 (Critical)
4 (H)     4    8    12   16   20
3 (M)     3    6    9    12   15 (High)
2 (L)     2    4    6    8    10
1 (R)     1    2    3    4    5
```

**Risk Categories:**
- **Green (1–5):** Low risk, accept, standard controls
- **Yellow (6–10):** Medium risk, monitor, targeted controls
- **Orange (12–15):** High risk, mitigate, senior oversight
- **Red (16–25):** Critical, urgent action, immediate mitigation

### Risk Assessment Process

**Step 1: Identify (Department-Led)**
- Department lists potential risks
- Assess process failures, systems, fraud scenarios

**Step 2: Score (Risk Team + Department)**
- Estimate impact and likelihood
- Consider existing controls
- Calculate residual risk

**Step 3: Mitigate (Department-Led)**
- Identify control gaps
- Implement targeted controls
- Assign responsibility and timeline

**Step 4: Monitor (Continuous)**
- Monitor key risk indicators (KRIs)
- Adjust controls if needed
- Escalate if risk increases

## Incident Management

**Incident = Unplanned event causing actual loss or regulatory breach**

### Incident Reporting

**Immediate Actions:**
- Report to manager immediately
- Record in incident log
- Escalate to Risk team if >£10k loss
- Escalate to Compliance if regulatory breach

**Information Captured:**
- Date, time, incident type
- Affected systems/processes
- Estimated impact
- Root cause (if known)
- Immediate containment steps

### Investigation

**Timeline:**
- Incidents <£10k: Investigated within 5 business days
- Incidents £10k–£100k: Investigated within 2 business days
- Incidents >£100k: Immediate escalation to CRO + CEO

**Investigation Elements:**
- What happened (detailed timeline)
- Root cause analysis
- Lessons learned
- Corrective action
- Prevention measures

### Escalation

**Incident Escalation Triggers:**

| Loss Amount | Escalation | Timeline |
|-------------|-----------|----------|
| <£1k | Department Manager | Report to Risk |
| £1k–£10k | Risk Manager | Report to CRO within 1 day |
| £10k–£100k | CRO + CEO | Report within 24 hours |
| >£100k | Board + Regulators | Immediate + regulatory notification |

## Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)

**KRIs = Early warning signals of emerging risk**

### Operational KRIs

| KRI | Target | Action if Exceeded |
|-----|--------|-------------------|
| Account opening errors | <0.5% | Review process, retrain staff |
| System availability | 99.8% | Investigate outage, remediate |
| Reconciliation variance | <£1k daily | Investigate, correct |
| Fraud detection rate | 97%+ | Tune fraud rules, investigate gaps |
| Compliance violations | 0 | Immediate remediation |
| Customer complaints | <2 per 1,000 accounts | Root cause analysis |

### Process KRIs

| Process | KRI | Target | Monitoring |
|---------|-----|--------|-----------|
| Account Opening | Error rate | <0.5% | Monthly |
| Payment Processing | Failed transactions | <0.1% | Daily |
| Fraud Detection | False positive rate | <2% | Weekly |
| AML Screening | Screening delay | <2 hours | Continuous |

## Risk Governance

### Risk Committee (see [[departments]] Board Risk Committee)

**Frequency:** Quarterly  
**Members:** CRO, CEO, CFO, Compliance Head, Internal Audit  
**Agenda:** Risk appetite, KRIs, incidents, control effectiveness  

### Department Risk Owners

**Responsibility:**
- Identify risks in area
- Implement controls
- Monitor KRIs
- Report to Risk team
- Escalate incidents

### Risk Team

**Responsibility:**
- Maintain risk framework
- Aggregate risk assessment
- Monitor trends
- Report to Board Risk Committee
- Regulatory liaison

## Risk Appetite

**FirstUK Bank's Risk Appetite = Conservative**

**Operational Loss Tolerance:**
- Annual operational losses: <£50k (0.15% of revenue)
- Major incident acceptance: <1 per year
- Compliance violations: 0 tolerated
- Fraud loss: <£100k annually
