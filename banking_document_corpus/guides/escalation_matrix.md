## Purpose

The Escalation Matrix defines clear escalation paths for issues, incidents, and decisions based on severity, type, and financial impact. Ensures rapid resolution, appropriate authority, and transparent accountability.

---

## Escalation Levels & Approvals

### Level 1: Frontline Resolution

**Authority:** Customer Service Staff, Branch Staff, Account Managers  
**Approval Limit:** <£500  
**Examples:**
- Account inquiry resolution
- Simple fee reversal request
- Standard product inquiry
- Lost card replacement ($15 fee)
- Password reset

**Response Time:** Same day (within 24 hours)

**Escalation Condition:** If customer unhappy or issue complex → Escalate to Level 2

---

### Level 2: Supervisor/Manager Resolution

**Authority:** Supervisor, Branch Manager, Complaints Handler  
**Approval Limit:** £500–£5,000  
**Examples:**
- Complaint investigation
- Non-standard fee reversal
- Account exception handling
- Customer retention offer
- Process waiver requests

**Response Time:** 1–3 business days

**Escalation Condition:** If unresolved or >£5k impact → Escalate to Level 3

---

### Level 3: Department Head Resolution

**Authority:** Department Head (e.g., Head of Retail Banking, Head of Compliance)  
**Approval Limit:** £5,000–£50,000  
**Examples:**
- Significant complaint resolution (substantial refund)
- Regulatory exception requests
- High-value fraud investigation
- Product exception request
- Vendor/partnership dispute
- AML/KYC escalation (high-risk customer decision)

**Response Time:** 3–5 business days

**Escalation Condition:** If unresolved or >£50k impact → Escalate to Level 4

---

### Level 4: Executive Committee Resolution

**Authority:** CEO, CFO, CRO, COO (Executive Committee)  
**Approval Limit:** £50,000–£500,000  
**Examples:**
- Major financial loss (>£50k)
- Significant regulatory breach
- Reputational crisis
- Major vendor issue
- Strategic exception
- Large fraud investigation

**Response Time:** 24–48 hours

**Escalation Condition:** If unresolved or >£500k impact → Escalate to Level 5 (Board)

---

### Level 5: Board Resolution

**Authority:** Board of Directors  
**Approval Limit:** >£500,000  
**Examples:**
- Major financial loss (>£500k)
- Critical regulatory breach
- Reputational crisis
- Legal liability
- Strategic decisions
- License/charter risk
- Systemic operational failure

**Response Time:** 24–72 hours (emergency meeting if urgent)

## Issue-Specific Escalation Paths

### Customer Complaints

**Severity: Low (Minor service issue)**
- Initial Handler: Customer Service Staff
- Level: 1–2
- Timeline: 24 hours
- If unresolved: Escalate to Complaints Manager (Level 3)

**Severity: High (Significant financial loss)**
- Initial Handler: Complaints Manager
- Level: 3–4
- Timeline: 24–48 hours
- Regulatory implication: Notify Compliance
- If >£100k: Board notification

**Severity: Critical (Regulatory/reputational breach)**
- Initial Handler: Head of Customer Service + Compliance Head
- Level: 4–5
- Timeline: 24 hours
- FCA notification: Within 24 hours (if required)
- Board: Immediate notification

### Fraud Incidents

**Fraud Amount: <£1,000**
- Handler: Financial Crime Team
- Level: 2
- Timeline: 24 hours
- Action: Investigate, block if ongoing, reimburse if customer fraud

**Fraud Amount: £1,000–£10,000**
- Handler: Financial Crime Team + CRO
- Level: 3
- Timeline: 24 hours
- Action: Investigation + law enforcement contact (if warranted)
- Reimbursement: Within 10 days

**Fraud Amount: >£10,000**
- Handler: CRO + CEO
- Level: 4–5
- Timeline: 4 hours
- Action: Investigation, law enforcement notification, Board update
- Reimbursement: Within 10 days
- Regulatory: May require SAR filing/notification

### Operational Incidents/System Outages

**Duration: <1 hour**
- Handler: IT Operations
- Level: 1–2
- Customer Impact: Minimal
- Timeline: Resolve within 1 hour
- Reporting: Log incident, no escalation if <1 hour

**Duration: 1–4 hours**
- Handler: IT Operations + CTO
- Level: 2–3
- Customer Impact: Significant
- Timeline: Resolve within 4 hours
- Reporting: Escalate to CRO + COO after 2 hours
- Communication: Update customers if >2 hours

**Duration: >4 hours**
- Handler: CTO + CEO
- Level: 4–5
- Customer Impact: Critical
- Timeline: Immediate response + hourly updates
- Reporting: Executive committee meeting (if >4 hours)
- Regulatory: May require FCA notification
- Board: Update (if >8 hours)

### Compliance Violations

**Severity: Policy Violation**
- Handler: Compliance + Department Manager
- Level: 2
- Action: Counseling, corrective action plan
- Timeline: Corrective action within 30 days

**Severity: Regulatory Rule Breach (Low Impact)**
- Handler: Compliance + CRO
- Level: 3
- Action: Immediate remediation, internal notification
- Timeline: Remediate within 5 days
- Reporting: Document remediation

**Severity: Significant Regulatory Breach**
- Handler: CRO + CEO + Legal
- Level: 4–5
- Action: Immediate escalation, regulatory contact
- Timeline: 24 hours
- Reporting: FCA/PRA notification (per rules)
- Board: Immediate notification
- Remediation: Root cause analysis + prevention plan

### AML/KYC Issues

**Issue: Missed KYC Documentation**
- Handler: Compliance
- Level: 2
- Action: Obtain documentation immediately
- Timeline: Same day (before processing account)

**Issue: PEP/Sanctions Hit**
- Handler: Compliance + Financial Crime
- Level: 3–4
- Action: Account freeze immediately
- Timeline: Within 4 hours
- Reporting: NCA notification (Office of Financial Sanctions Implementation)

**Issue: Suspicious Transaction Pattern**
- Handler: Financial Crime + Compliance
- Level: 2–3
- Action: Enhanced monitoring, potential SAR
- Timeline: Investigate within 5 days
- SAR Filing: Within 30 days (if warranted)

## Escalation Best Practices

### When to Escalate

**Escalate Immediately If:**
- Financial impact exceeds your approval limit
- Regulatory/compliance implication
- Reputational risk
- Customer relationship at risk
- You lack authority/expertise
- Issue is unclear/complex
- Unresolved after 24 hours

### How to Escalate

1. **Document Issue:** Summary, timeline, financial impact, efforts so far
2. **Contact Escalation Authority:** Email + phone call (don't wait)
3. **Provide Context:** Background, options considered, recommendation
4. **Wait for Response:** Authority may ask questions (respond promptly)
5. **Implement Decision:** Once authority decides, execute immediately
6. **Update Stakeholders:** Inform customer, team, manager

### Escalation Etiquette

- ✅ DO provide complete context (don't make escalation authority investigate)
- ✅ DO include recommendation (show you've thought it through)
- ✅ DO be responsive (answer authority questions quickly)
- ✅ DO follow decision (implement without second-guessing)
- ❌ DON'T escalate until you've tried resolution at your level
- ❌ DON'T escalate again if already decided (only if circumstances change)
- ❌ DON'T escalate trivial issues (know your authority limit)
