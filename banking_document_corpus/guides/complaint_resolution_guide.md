## Purpose

Help customers understand the complaint process and their rights, including how to raise complaints, what to expect, and how to escalate if unsatisfied.

---

## What Is a Complaint?

A complaint is any expression of dissatisfaction about our service, products, or actions that requires a response.

**Examples:**
- "My transfer failed and wasn't refunded"
- "I wasn't informed about a rate change"
- "I was treated poorly in a branch"
- "Your website is difficult to use"
- "A Direct Debit was taken without authorization"

---

## Your Rights

**8-Day Response Guarantee**  
We will respond to your complaint within **8 calendar days** of receipt.

**Free Ombudsman Escalation**  
If you're unhappy with our response, you can escalate to the Financial Ombudsman Service (free and independent).

**No Retaliation**  
We will not penalize you for making a complaint.

**Full Investigation**  
We will thoroughly investigate your complaint and provide a clear explanation.

## How to Raise a Complaint

### Option 1: Call Us
**Phone:** 0800-555-2265  
**Hours:** Mon–Fri 08:00–20:00, Sat 09:00–17:00  
**What to Say:** Explain your issue clearly and ask to speak with a supervisor if needed.

### Option 2: Email
**Email:** support@firstuk.bank  
**Subject:** "I want to lodge a formal complaint"  
**What to Include:**
- Your name and account number
- Date of the issue
- Clear description of what happened
- What you think went wrong
- How you'd like it resolved

### Option 3: Visit a Branch
**Locations:** Any of our 12 UK branches  
**What to Do:** Ask to speak with a manager or use the complaint form.

### Option 4: Online Complaint Form
**Website:** www.firstuk.bank/complaints  
**Process:** Fill out form and submit securely.

## What Happens Next: The 4-Step Process

### Step 1: Acknowledgment (Immediate)

Within 1 business day of receipt, you'll receive:
- **Complaint Reference Number** (e.g., EXC-00012345)
- **Acknowledgment Email/Letter** confirming we've received your complaint
- **Name of Your Investigator** (if available)

**Keep your reference number safe** — use it in all future correspondence.

### Step 2: Investigation (Days 1–7)

Our investigation team will:
- Review all relevant documents and records
- Interview staff involved (if necessary)
- Contact third parties (if needed, e.g., merchant, other bank)
- Assess what went wrong
- Determine if compensation is warranted

**You may be contacted** for additional information if needed.

### Step 3: Decision (By Day 8)

You'll receive a response letter explaining:
- **Our findings** (what we discovered)
- **Our decision** (upheld, partially upheld, or rejected)
- **Our action** (refund, explanation, other resolution)
- **Compensation (if awarded)**

### Step 4: Resolution & Escalation (If Needed)

**If you accept our decision:**
- Complaint closed
- Refund processed (if applicable)
- No further action

**If you disagree with our decision:**
- You have **6 months** to escalate to Financial Ombudsman
- You can escalate anytime without further correspondence with us

## Common Complaint Scenarios & Resolutions

### Scenario 1: Failed Transfer (Not Refunded)

**Your Issue:** "I tried to transfer £500 but it failed. Money was taken from my account."

**What We Investigate:**
- Did the transfer actually complete or fail?
- If failed, was the money debited or not?
- Why did it fail?

**Possible Outcomes:**
- **Authorized:** No fee charged (system error); refund + interest
- **Insufficient funds:** Explain why it failed; no refund
- **Invalid recipient:** Explain error; refund if our fault

**Typical Resolution Time:** 2–3 business days

---

### Scenario 2: Wrong Interest Charged

**Your Issue:** "I was charged the wrong interest rate."

**What We Investigate:**
- What rate were you supposed to receive?
- What rate were you actually charged?
- For how long?
- Did this benefit or harm you?

**Possible Outcomes:**
- **Our error:** Recalculate interest; refund difference + additional interest for inconvenience
- **Policy error:** Explain policy; refund if error caused loss
- **No error:** Explain the rate and why it's correct

**Typical Resolution Time:** 3–5 business days

---

### Scenario 3: Unauthorized Direct Debit

**Your Issue:** "I didn't authorize this Direct Debit, and £150 was taken."

**What We Investigate:**
- Is there a signed mandate?
- Did you actually authorize it?
- When was it authorized?
- Is the amount correct per mandate?

**Possible Outcomes:**
- **Genuinely unauthorized:** Refund £150 + interest within 10 days (Direct Debit Guarantee)
- **You authorized but forgot:** Explanation; option to cancel going forward
- **Amount error:** Refund overage only

**Typical Resolution Time:** 5–10 business days

---

### Scenario 4: Poor Customer Service

**Your Issue:** "Staff member was rude to me in the branch."

**What We Investigate:**
- When did this occur?
- Who was the staff member?
- What was said/done?
- How did it impact you?

**Possible Outcomes:**
- **Service failure confirmed:** Apology; staff retraining; compensation (£20–£100 depending on severity)
- **Misunderstanding:** Explanation of staff's actions; clarification
- **Not substantiated:** Explanation of our findings

**Typical Resolution Time:** 5–7 business days

---

### Scenario 5: Account Restrictions

**Your Issue:** "My account was frozen without explanation."

**What We Investigate:**
- Why was it frozen? (Regulatory reason? Fraud hold? Other?)
- Was proper notice given?
- Was the freeze justified?

**Possible Outcomes:**
- **Justified:** Explain reason (e.g., KYC compliance, fraud investigation); timeline for resolution
- **Unjustified:** Unfreeze immediately; apologize; compensation
- **Process error:** Unfreeze; improve process

**Typical Resolution Time:** 3–5 business days

## Escalation to Financial Ombudsman Service

### When Can You Escalate?

You can escalate if:
- You're unhappy with our decision
- We didn't respond within 8 days
- We never acknowledged receipt

### How to Escalate

**Financial Ombudsman Service:**
- **Phone:** 0800 023 4567
- **Website:** www.financialombudsman.org.uk
- **Email:** complaint@financialombudsman.org.uk
- **Address:** Exchange Tower, London, E14 9SR

**What You Need:**
- Your FirstUK complaint reference number
- Our decision letter
- Any relevant correspondence
- Clear explanation of why you disagree

### What They'll Do

The Ombudsman will:
- Review your complaint independently
- Contact FirstUK for additional information
- Issue a decision (binding on us, non-binding on you)
- Make an award (compensation) if they agree with you
- Handle everything FREE of charge

**Timeline:** Typically 3–6 months

## Complaint Compensation

### What We Can Offer

**Financial Compensation:**
- Refunds (for financial loss)
- Interest (for delayed refunds)
- Goodwill payments (£20–£100+)

**Non-Financial Remedies:**
- Apology
- Explanation
- Service improvements
- Policy changes

### Compensation Guidelines

| Issue Severity | Compensation Range |
|---|---|
| Minor (inconvenience, slow response) | £20–£50 |
| Moderate (financial loss <£100, service failure) | £50–£150 |
| Significant (financial loss £100–£500, multiple failures) | £150–£300 |
| Major (financial loss >£500, serious service failure) | £300+ (per FOS guidance) |

## Complaint Prevention Tips

**To Avoid Issues:**
1. **Verify details** before making transfers (account number, sort code)
2. **Keep records** of all communications
3. **Monitor your account** regularly for unauthorized activity
4. **Report issues quickly** — don't wait weeks
5. **Use secure passwords** and enable 2FA
6. **Ask questions** if something seems wrong
