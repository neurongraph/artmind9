## Executive Summary

FirstUK Bank organizes around 12 core departments, each with defined roles, responsibilities, and interfaces. This document provides the organizational blueprint for departmental functions, reporting lines, and cross-functional collaboration.

---

## 1. Retail Banking Department

**Department Head:** Director of Retail Banking  
**Reports to:** CEO  
**Headcount:** 8  
**Key Locations:** Head Office (3), Branches (5)  

### Mission

Deliver consumer banking products and services, drive customer acquisition, and maximize customer lifetime value.

### Key Functions

#### Sales & Onboarding
- Customer acquisition campaigns
- Account opening support
- Digital onboarding (online, mobile)
- Branch-based sales

**Manager:** Manager of Sales & Onboarding

#### Customer Service & Retention
- Frontline support (phone, email, chat, branch)
- Complaint resolution (first line)
- Customer retention programs
- Service quality assurance

**Manager:** Manager of Customer Service

#### Branch Operations
- Daily branch operations management
- Cash handling and reconciliation
- Customer meeting facilitation
- Local marketing

**Location Manager:** At each branch (see [[branches]])

| Account Opening Error Rate | <0.5% | 0.3% |

### Cross-Functional Interfaces

- **Technology:** Online banking issues, app bugs

**Location:** Head Office (2), Branches (1)  

### Mission

Deliver responsive, empathetic customer support and resolve complaints efficiently.

### Key Functions

- Phone support (0800-555-2265)
- Email support (support@firstuk.bank)
- Online chat support

**Hours:** Monday–Friday 08:00–20:00, Saturday 09:00–17:00

#### Complaint Resolution

**Process:**
1. First-line resolution (Retail Banking or Customer Service)

## 5. Financial Crime Department

**Department Head:** Head of Financial Crime  
**Reports to:** Chief Risk Officer  
**Headcount:** 2  
**Location:** Head Office  

### Mission

Detect, prevent, and report financial crime, including money laundering, fraud, and sanctions violations.

### Key Functions

#### AML/KYC Compliance
- Customer Due Diligence (CDD) — initial verification at account opening
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) — for higher-risk customers
- Ongoing Monitoring — transaction pattern analysis
- Customer Screening — sanctions list, PEP screening

**See:** AML Policy (AML-POL-002), KYC SOP (KYC-SOP-001)

#### Suspicious Activity Reporting
- Transaction monitoring (automated)
- Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) to NCA
- Regulatory reporting

#### Fraud Prevention
- Fraud detection rules (rules engine in [[systems]])
- Card fraud monitoring
- Account takeover prevention
- Customer authentication reviews

**See:** Fraud Policy (FRAUD-POL-007)

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Count |
|--------|-------|
| Customers Screened (monthly) | 50 |
| High-Risk Alerts | 5–10 per month |
| SARs Filed (annual) | 8–12 |
| Fraud Claims (monthly) | 2–3 |
| Fraud Prevention Rate | 97%+ |

### Regulatory Requirements

- FCA COBS Part 10 (Anti-money laundering)
- Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 (POCA)
- Terrorism Act 2000
- GDPR (data handling)

### Cross-Functional Interfaces

- **Retail Banking:** Customer onboarding, due diligence
- **Compliance:** Regulatory reporting, audit support
- **Operations:** Transaction review, exception handling
- **Technology:** Fraud detection systems, monitoring tools

## 7. Risk Department

**Department Head:** Chief Risk Officer  
**Reports to:** Board of Directors  
**Headcount:** 2  
**Location:** Head Office  

### Mission

Identify, measure, and manage enterprise risks to protect the bank's capital and franchise.

### Key Functions

#### Credit Risk Management
- Lending decisions (for mortgages)
- Credit scoring and assessment
- Loan monitoring
- Delinquency management

#### Operational Risk Management
- Risk assessment (processes, systems, people)
- Risk scenario analysis
- Loss data collection
- Operational risk KPIs

#### Market & Liquidity Risk
- Interest rate risk
- Liquidity management
- Treasury oversight

### Risk Assessment

**Risk Categories:**
- Credit Risk (mortgages, overdrafts)
- Operational Risk (process failures, fraud)
- Compliance Risk (regulatory breaches)
- Reputational Risk (brand damage, complaints)
- Strategic Risk (business model changes)

### Key Risk Appetite

| Risk Type | Appetite |
|-----------|----------|
| Credit Loss Ratio | <0.5% of loan book |
| Operational Loss | <£10k per annum |
| Regulatory Capital Ratio (Pillar 1) | >15% |
| Complaint Ratio | <2 per 1,000 accounts |

### Risk Committee (see [[organisation_model]])

**Frequency:** Quarterly  
**Chair:** Board Risk Committee Chair (independent director)  
**Members:** Board directors, CRO, CFO, Compliance Head  
**Agenda:** Risk appetite, capital strategy, risk metrics, audit findings

### Cross-Functional Interfaces

- **Product Management:** Product risk assessment
- **Retail Banking:** Customer credit decisions
- **Operations:** Process risk identification
- **Finance:** Capital management, stress testing
- **Internal Audit:** Risk and control testing
