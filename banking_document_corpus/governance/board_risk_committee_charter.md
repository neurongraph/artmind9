## Purpose

The Board Risk Committee (BRC) is a standing committee of the Board of Directors responsible for overseeing FirstUK Bank's enterprise risk management, capital adequacy, and internal controls.

---

## Charter

### 1. Committee Composition

**Members:**
- Independent Non-Executive Director (Chair)
- 2–3 Independent Non-Executive Directors
- Chief Risk Officer (executive, non-voting in deliberations)
- Chief Financial Officer (executive, non-voting in deliberations)

**Minimum Independence:**
- Majority of members must be independent (per FCA/PRA definition)
- Chair must be independent
- No executive directors as voting members (except as observers)

**Committee Secretary:**
- Appointed by Board
- Provides administrative support
- Maintains records, meeting minutes

### 2. Authority & Responsibilities

**Strategic Risk Oversight:**
- Approve enterprise risk management framework
- Review and approve Risk Appetite Statement
- Monitor risk-weighted capital adequacy
- Assess emerging risks to the bank's strategy

**Capital Management:**
- Monitor capital ratios (Pillar 1 minimum >15%)
- Approve capital raising/deployment decisions
- Stress testing and scenario analysis
- Capital distribution (dividends, buybacks, if applicable)

**Credit Risk:**
- Review credit strategy and risk limits
- Monitor loan portfolio quality
- Assess borrower concentration risk
- Review major lending decisions (mortgages >£500k)

**Operational Risk:**
- Review operational risk incidents
- Assess control environment
- Monitor operational risk KRIs
- Approve risk mitigation strategies

**Compliance & Regulatory:**
- Monitor regulatory compliance
- Review audit findings and remediation
- Assess regulatory relationships
- Approve regulatory responses

**Fraud & Financial Crime:**
- Review fraud incidents and investigations
- Monitor AML/KYC effectiveness
- Review SAR (Suspicious Activity Report) filings
- Assess anti-fraud controls

**Cybersecurity & Technology:**
- Review cybersecurity risks
- Monitor system availability
- Assess major technology investments
- Review incident response procedures

### 3. Reporting Lines

**Reports to:**
- Full Board of Directors (Chair reports key decisions/issues)

**Receives from:**
- Chief Risk Officer (risk management updates)
- Chief Financial Officer (capital, financial risk)
- Compliance Head (regulatory, AML/KYC)
- Head of Internal Audit (audit findings)
- Chief Technology Officer (technology risk)

**Accountability:**
- Committee Chair accountable to Board
- Board accountable to regulators (FCA, PRA)
- Regulators may meet with committee directly

### 4. Meeting Schedule & Procedures

**Frequency:**
- Quarterly (minimum) — scheduled meetings
- Emergency meetings as needed (within 24 hours if critical risk)

**Attendance:**
- All voting members expected to attend (min. 3 per meeting)
- Executive attendees as needed
- Advisors as needed (legal, external risk consultants)
- External auditors invited (at least annually)
- Internal auditors invited (at least quarterly)

**Agenda Setting:**
- Chair sets agenda (input from CRO, CFO, Compliance)
- Standing items: Risk appetite, capital ratios, incidents, KRIs
- Advance notice: Agenda and papers provided 5 days before meeting

**Meeting Minutes:**
- Recorded by Committee Secretary
- Minutes approved by Chair within 5 days
- Distributed to all Board members
- Retained for 7 years

**Confidentiality:**
- Committee discussions confidential (unless public disclosure required)
- Executive session (without management) held quarterly
- Minutes protected as Board materials (legal privilege)

### 5. Specific Responsibilities

**Risk Appetite Statement:**
- Review annually
- Align with strategic objectives
- Define tolerances (capital, losses, regulatory)
- Communicate to business units

**Capital Planning:**
- Annual capital plan review
- Stress testing scenarios (recession, market shock, fraud)
- Capital ratio monitoring (quarterly)
- Dividend/distribution approval (if applicable)

**Audit Oversight:**
- Review Internal Audit plan (annual)
- Monitor audit findings and remediation
- Assess Internal Audit independence
- Approve Internal Audit budget

**Regulatory Engagement:**
- Receive FCA/PRA feedback
- Approve regulatory responses
- Discuss regulatory concerns/orders
- Plan remediation for findings

**Board Education:**
- Annual risk management training
- Market risk overview (quarterly)
- Emerging risk briefings
- Regulatory change updates

### 6. Decision Authority

**Decisions Made by Committee:**
- Risk Appetite Statement (approval)
- Major risk policy changes (e.g., credit policy)
- Risk limits and KRIs (approval)
- Risk remediation strategies (approval if >£500k spend)
- Large lending decisions (mortgages >£500k)
- Stress testing scenarios

**Decisions Requiring Board Approval:**
- Capital raising/deployment
- Regulatory capital requirements compliance
- Significant strategic risk changes
- Major acquisitions (if applicable)
- Large financial provisions/write-offs

**Executive Authority (Within Risk Appetite):**
- Operational risk incidents <£10k
- Credit decisions within approved limits
- Fraud reimbursement <£5,000
- AML/KYC approvals (standard)
- Technology investments <£100k

### 7. Committee Self-Assessment

**Annual Assessment:**
- Committee effectiveness review
- Member skills and experience assessment
- Meetings and processes evaluation
- Recommendations for improvements

**Areas Assessed:**
- Committee composition (skills, independence, diversity)
- Meeting effectiveness (time use, agenda relevance)
- Quality of information/papers provided
- Engagement with management
- Stakeholder communication

**Actions:**
- Results reported to Board
- Training identified and provided
- Process improvements implemented

### 8. Reporting to Regulators

**FCA/PRA Correspondence:**
- Risk Committee Chair may meet with regulators directly
- Committee updates sent to regulators (annually or as requested)
- Regulatory concerns/orders escalated immediately
- Regulatory feedback discussed in committee

**Regulatory Requirements:**
- Committee composition meets regulatory standards
- Meeting frequency meets regulatory expectations
- Risk oversight sufficiently detailed per regulations
- Documentation complete and retained
