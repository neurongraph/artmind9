## Purpose

The Internal Audit Charter defines FirstUK Bank's Internal Audit function's independence, authority, scope, and responsibility for providing independent assurance over risk management and control effectiveness.

---

## Charter

### 1. Definition

**Internal Audit:** An independent, objective function that evaluates the adequacy and effectiveness of FirstUK Bank's risk management, control, and governance processes.

**Independence:** Internal Audit reports directly to Board Audit Committee, not to management, ensuring impartiality.

---

### 2. Mission Statement

Internal Audit adds value to FirstUK Bank by:
- Evaluating effectiveness of risk management and internal controls
- Providing objective assurance and recommendations
- Promoting accountability and transparency
- Supporting management in achieving objectives
- Identifying opportunities for operational improvement

---

### 3. Authority & Scope

### 3.1 Authority

**Unrestricted Access:**
- Full access to all records, systems, and premises
- Authority to interview any employee or contractor
- Right to access Board Audit Committee directly (no management filter)
- Ability to observe any business activity or process
- Authority to hire external specialists (with approval)

**No Authority to:**
- Manage business operations
- Make business decisions
- Implement controls (advise, not execute)
- Override management decisions
- Direct departmental activities

### 3.2 Audit Scope

**In Scope (All Areas Audited):**
- Business processes (account opening, payment processing, etc.)
- Financial controls (reconciliation, GL controls, etc.)
- Operational controls (segregation of duties, authorization, etc.)
- Compliance controls (KYC, AML, regulatory)
- Technology controls (access, change management, backups)
- Risk management (risk assessment, mitigation)
- Fraud and misconduct investigations
- Third-party vendors and service providers
- Regulatory compliance

**Out of Scope (Not Audited):**
- Board decisions (governance oversight, not audit)
- Strategic decisions (unless control/compliance concern)
- Items covered by external auditors (coordination, no duplication)

### 3.3 Audit Standards

Internal Audit follows:
- **International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing (ISPPIA)**
- **IIA Code of Ethics** (Integrity, Objectivity, Confidentiality, Competency)
- **FCA and PRA Expectations** for internal audit function
- **FirstUK Bank Audit Charter** (this document)

---

### 4. Reporting Structure

**Internal Reporting:**
- Reports functionally to Board Audit Committee (primary reporting line)
- Reports administratively to Chief Operating Officer (for budgeting, HR, facilities)
- Direct access to Chief Executive Officer (escalation)
- Board Audit Committee meetings quarterly minimum

**External Reporting:**
- FCA/PRA may communicate with Head of Internal Audit directly
- Regulatory examination findings shared with Board Audit Committee
- External audit coordination (no duplication of effort)

**Organizational Independence:**
- Internal Audit separate from operational departments
- No operational responsibilities or conflicts
- Cannot audit areas where led/managed audit team
- Rotates staff every 5–7 years (prevent familiarity)

---

### 5. Audit Plan & Priorities

### 5.1 Annual Audit Plan

**Development Process:**
- Risk assessment (identify highest-risk areas)
- Stakeholder input (department heads, management)
- Regulatory expectations (FCA, PRA guidance)
- Known issues from prior audits
- Board Audit Committee approval

**Plan Components:**
- Planned audits (risk-based)
- Estimated hours per audit
- Sequencing/timeline
- Resource requirements
- Budget allocation

**Plan Flexibility:**
- Plan adjusted for emerging risks/urgent issues
- Special audits conducted as needed (regulatory concern, fraud investigation)
- In-year changes reported to Board Audit Committee

### 5.2 Audit Prioritization

**Risk-Based Prioritization:**
- **High Risk (Annual Audit):** AML/KYC, Payment Processing, Fraud Detection, Technology Controls
- **Medium Risk (Biennial Audit):** Complaint Handling, Account Opening, Operational Risk, Compliance
- **Low Risk (Triennial Audit):** Facilities, Payroll, Travel & Expenses

**2026 Audit Plan (Illustrative):**
1. Account Opening & KYC (Regulatory focus)
2. Payment Processing (Critical process)
3. Fraud Detection (High-risk)
4. IT General Controls (Annual standard)
5. Operational Risk (Annual standard)
6. Compliance Controls (Annual standard)

---

### 6. Audit Process

### 6.1 Audit Methodology

**Planning Phase:**
- Define audit scope and objectives
- Risk assessment for target process
- Interview key personnel
- Document process workflows
- Identify key controls

**Fieldwork Phase:**
- Test key controls (sample transaction review)
- Conduct interviews
- Analyze data and findings
- Document evidence
- Assess control effectiveness

**Reporting Phase:**
- Draft audit report (findings, conclusions)
- Present findings to management
- Management response and action plan
- Final report issued
- Executive summary to Board Audit Committee

**Follow-Up Phase:**
- Monitor management's remediation progress
- Re-test key controls (if major findings)
- Quarterly status update to Board Audit Committee

### 6.2 Finding Classification

**Finding Severity:**

| Grade | Definition | Timeframe | Example |
|-------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Critical | Control failure causing financial loss/regulatory breach | Remediate within 30 days | KYC not completed on account |
| High | Control weakness with significant risk | Remediate within 90 days | Missing approvals on transactions |
| Medium | Control gap with moderate impact | Remediate within 180 days | Process manual (could automate) |
| Low | Control improvement opportunity | Remediate within 1 year | Outdated procedure, minor risk |

**Remediation Tracking:**
- Management submits action plan (target date, responsible party)
- Board Audit Committee reviews quarterly
- Internal Audit follows up (re-test controls)
- Closure when remediation verified

---

### 7. Audit Committee Oversight

**Board Audit Committee Responsibilities:**
- Approve annual audit plan
- Receive quarterly audit updates
- Review significant findings
- Monitor management remediation
- Approve audit budget
- Assess Internal Audit effectiveness
- Meet separately with Internal Audit (no management present)

**Meeting Frequency:**
- Quarterly (minimum)
- Emergency meetings as needed
- Executive session with Head of Internal Audit (quarterly)

---

### 8. Independence & Conflicts of Interest

### 8.1 Maintaining Independence

**Safeguards:**
- No operational responsibilities
- No decision-making authority
- No conflict of interest roles
- Separate reporting to Board Audit Committee
- Budget approved by Board (not management)
- Staff appraisals reviewed by Board chair

**Prohibited Activities:**
- Internal Audit staff cannot audit departments where recently managed
- Internal Audit staff cannot audit their own work
- Internal Audit staff cannot approve transactions or controls they audit
- Internal Audit cannot advise on strategy (can audit strategic decisions)

### 8.2 Conflict Resolution

**If Conflict Identified:**
- Escalate to Head of Internal Audit immediately
- Head of Internal Audit reports to Board Audit Committee
- Committee decides resolution (reassign audit, external auditor, etc.)
- Documentation of conflict and resolution

---

### 9. Resource Requirements

**Staffing:**
- Head of Internal Audit (1 FTE)
- Internal Auditors (1–2 FTE, depending on scope)
- Specialist consultants as needed (technology audit, forensics)

**Skills Required:**
- Accounting/financial controls knowledge
- Risk management understanding
- Process mapping and analysis
- IT controls knowledge (databases, systems)
- Regulatory compliance knowledge
- Communication skills (report writing)

**Development:**
- Auditor certifications (CIA, CISA, etc.)
- Continuous professional education
- Training budget allocated

**Budget:**
- Salaries and benefits (approved by Board)
- External consultants as needed
- Training and certifications
- Audit software and tools

---

### 10. External Audit Coordination

**Coordination with External Auditors:**
- Quarterly meetings to align audit plans
- No duplication of effort
- Share findings and recommendations
- Internal audit findings inform external audit risk assessment
- Joint discussions of significant issues

**Regulatory Audit Coordination:**
- FCA/PRA may conduct examinations
- Internal Audit provides cooperation and documentation
- Internal Audit findings shared with regulators (if requested)
- Board Audit Committee informed of regulatory feedback
