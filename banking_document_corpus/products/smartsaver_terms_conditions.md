If your account has:
- No activity (deposits or withdrawals) for 12 months, AND

Your account becomes "dormant." We will:
1. Send letter to last known address
2. Mark account as dormant (no impact on you)
3. Continue accruing interest
4. Maintain full access (deposit/withdrawal possible)

## 4. INTEREST

### 4.1 Interest Rates

Your account earns interest at a **variable rate** based on your balance tier:

| Tier | Balance | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | £0–£10,000 | 4.50% AER |
| 2 | £10,001–£50,000 | 4.70% AER |
| 3 | £50,001+ | 4.80% AER |

**AER** = Annual Equivalent Rate (shows the yearly interest)

### 4.2 Interest Calculation

- **Accrual:** Calculated daily, paid monthly
- **Payment Date:** Last business day of each month
- **Tiering:** Tiered rates apply to balance as of payment date

**Example (30-day month):**
- Opening balance: £10,000 (Tier 1: 4.50%)
- Interest accrual: £10,000 × 4.50% ÷ 365 days × 30 days = £36.99
- Paid on: Last business day of month

### 4.3 Rate Changes

- **Review Frequency:** Monthly
- **Basis:** Bank of England Base Rate + 2.5% margin (margin fixed)
- **Notice:** Email notification when rate changes
- **Effective:** 1st of following month
- **Your Choice:** Switch to different product if unhappy with rate

## 6. STANDING ORDERS & DIRECT DEBITS

### 6.1 Standing Orders

A standing order is a regular, fixed transfer you set up.

**Setup:**
- Specify payee, amount, frequency (weekly/monthly/etc.)
- Provide receiving account details
- Standing order executes on scheduled date

**Cancellation:**
- Cancel at any time (no notice period required)
- Cancellation effective immediately
- Future payments blocked

### 6.2 Direct Debits

A direct debit is an authorization for a biller to withdraw money.

**Setup:**
- Provide authorization (mandate) to biller
- Specify maximum amount
- Biller initiates withdrawals

**Protection (Direct Debit Guarantee):**
- Refund within 10 business days if unauthorized
- No questions asked
- Full protection under Direct Debit Guarantee

**Cancellation:**
- Request from bank (5 days notice usually)
- Or contact biller directly

## 7. JOINT ACCOUNT PROVISIONS

### 7.1 Joint & Several Liability

For joint accounts:
- **Both Signatories Liable:** Either account holder can withdraw full balance
- **Interest Split:** Shared equally (unless you specify otherwise)
- **Disputes:** Both must agree to account closure

### 7.2 Death of Account Holder

If one joint account holder dies:
- Surviving holder retains full account access
- Estate of deceased entitled to their share (50%)
- Bank requires death certificate and documentation

### 7.3 Divorce/Separation

If joint account holders separate:
- Both retain legal rights to full account balance
- Either can freeze/close account (may require court order)
- Bank cannot determine who "owns" what (civil matter)
- Recommend legal advice

### 9.2 Closure Process

1. **Request Received:** We verify your identity

3. **Standing Orders/DDs:** Cancelled (effective immediately)
