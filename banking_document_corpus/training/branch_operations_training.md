## Purpose

Train branch staff on daily operations, customer service, opening procedures, and branch management standards.

---

## PART 1: Daily Branch Operations

### Opening Procedures (09:00 AM)

**30 Minutes Before Opening:**

1. **Physical Security**
   - [ ] Check entrance/exit doors locked
   - [ ] Disarm alarm system (code: [SECURITY CODE])
   - [ ] Check for any signs of break-in
   - [ ] Turn on lights and CCTV

2. **Cash Handling**
   - [ ] Count cash in vault (report to manager)
   - [ ] Verify amount matches overnight report
   - [ ] Load cash drawers for tellers (standard: £5,000 per drawer)
   - [ ] Record drawer assignments
   - [ ] Lock vault

3. **System Startup**
   - [ ] Boot up computers
   - [ ] Wait for systems to come online
   - [ ] Check system status dashboard (green = ready)
   - [ ] Log into teller systems with credentials
   - [ ] Verify ability to access customer accounts

4. **Branch Readiness**
   - [ ] Check waiting area (seating, magazines, temperature)
   - [ ] Verify reception area is clean
   - [ ] Check bathrooms are clean and stocked
   - [ ] Verify appointment calendar is loaded
   - [ ] Confirm staff are at their desks by 08:50 AM

**9:00 AM - Ready for Customers**

---

### During Business Hours (09:00–17:00)

**Hourly Checks:**

- [ ] Customer queue monitored (>10 people = call backup)
- [ ] All teller stations staffed
- [ ] Cash drawer balances monitored
- [ ] Systems running smoothly
- [ ] No customer complaints pending

**Continuous:**
- [ ] Greet customers within 2 minutes
- [ ] Handle transactions with accuracy
- [ ] Follow KYC/AML procedures for new accounts
- [ ] Resolve issues professionally
- [ ] Maintain cash drawer security

---

### Closing Procedures (17:00 PM)

**Last 30 Minutes:**

1. **Customer Management**
   - [ ] Last admission: 16:55 PM
   - [ ] Announce closing: "We close in 10 minutes"
   - [ ] Do not start new transactions after 16:55 PM

2. **Cash Handling**
   - [ ] Collect cash from all teller drawers
   - [ ] Count and verify (must match previous count)
   - [ ] Secure cash in vault
   - [ ] Count and seal cash for night deposit (if >£10,000)
   - [ ] Record closing balance
   - [ ] Lock vault

3. **System Shutdown**
   - [ ] Log out of all customer systems
   - [ ] Verify no transactions pending
   - [ ] Shut down computers gracefully
   - [ ] Wait for systems to fully shut down
   - [ ] Verify no sensitive data on screens

4. **Physical Closure**
   - [ ] Collect all customer files
   - [ ] File papers in secure location
   - [ ] Empty and shred trash
   - [ ] Lock all office doors
   - [ ] Turn off lights
   - [ ] Verify entrance/exit doors locked
   - [ ] Arm alarm system (code: [SECURITY CODE])
   - [ ] Record closure time and staff names

**Manager Sign-Off:** Verify branch closed properly and all cash reconciled

## PART 2: Customer Service Standards

### Customer Greeting

**Upon Arrival:**
- Greet within 2 minutes: "Good morning/afternoon, welcome to FirstUK Bank"
- Smile, make eye contact
- Ask how you can help
- Note customer mood (upset? urgent?)

### Waiting Customer Management

**If Wait > 5 Minutes:**
- Apologize: "Sorry for the wait, we'll be with you shortly"
- Offer refreshment: "Can I get you a coffee or water?"
- Keep updated: "About 3 more minutes"

**If Wait > 15 Minutes:**
- Escalate to manager
- Offer to schedule appointment
- Take contact info for callback

### Handling Difficult Customers

**Angry Customer:**
1. Listen without interrupting
2. Acknowledge their emotion: "I understand you're frustrated"
3. Apologize: "I'm sorry we let you down"
4. Explain next steps: "Here's what we'll do..."
5. Follow through immediately

**Escalation Triggers:**
- Customer raises voice or uses profanity
- Complaint involves >£1,000
- Customer requests manager
- Regulatory/compliance issue
- Potential fraud

**Action:** Stop transaction, call manager immediately

## PART 3: Account Opening in Branch

### Complete Process (45 Minutes)

**Step 1: Discovery (5 min)**
- Welcome customer
- Understand product need (savings? checking?)
- Recommend product
- Explain features and benefits

**Step 2: Application (10 min)**
- Complete application form
- Collect: Full name, DOB, address, phone, email
- Verify information
- Obtain signature

**Step 3: KYC Verification (15 min)**
- Request photo ID (UK Passport or Driving License)
- Examine original (check for validity, tampering)
- Photocopy and file
- Request proof of address (utility bill, council tax, bank statement)
- Photocopy and file
- Record: "ID verified: [Type], [Document Number]"

**Step 4: AML Screening (5 min)**
- Run customer through OFAC/EU/HMT screening
- Check result (should be clear)
- If hit: Escalate to Compliance
- Document result

**Step 5: Account Creation (5 min)**
- Teller inputs data into system
- System creates account
- Generate account number
- Print welcome pack
- Provide debit card application (if applicable)

**Step 6: Card Management (5 min)**
- If card ordered: Show 3–5 day timeline
- If card not ordered: Offer to order
- Explain card features and security

**Step 7: Welcome & Close (5 min)**
- Explain online banking login process
- Provide initial password
- Arrange first deposit method
- Offer help with first transfer/payment
- Thank customer, provide contact card

## PART 4: Common Branch Transactions

### Cash Deposit

**Process:**
1. Verify customer account
2. Count cash in front of customer
3. Verify count matches their receipt
4. Update account in system
5. Provide receipt
6. Close transaction

**Acceptable Cash:** Notes and coins in any condition  
**Suspicious Activity:** >£1,000 in cash (file CTR, see compliance)

---

### Cash Withdrawal

**Process:**
1. Verify customer identity (account card or ID)
2. Verify account has funds
3. Withdrawal limit: £5,000 max (above requires manager approval + notice)
4. Count cash carefully
5. Provide receipt
6. Close transaction

**Suspicious Activity:** >£1,000 withdrawal (file CTR, see compliance)

---

### Account Closure

**Process:**
1. Verify customer identity
2. Confirm closure request
3. Calculate final balance (pending interest, fees)
4. Print final statement
5. Arrange payout method (transfer, cheque, cash)
6. Deactivate account in system
7. Provide closure receipt

---

### Standing Order Setup

**Process:**
1. Obtain recipient details (name, account number, sort code)
2. Confirm payment amount and frequency
3. Confirm start date
4. Enter in system
5. Generate confirmation
6. Provide receipt

---

### Direct Debit Mandate Collection

**Process:**
1. Obtain signed DD mandate form
2. Verify account details
3. Verify biller DDIC
4. File mandate securely
5. Notify biller of receipt (automated)
6. Provide customer copy

## PART 5: Security Procedures

### Cash Security

**Vault Access:**
- Only manager has vault code
- Two-person rule: Manager + teller together
- Never leave vault open unattended
- Count cash on secure counter (not desk)

**Teller Drawers:**
- Locked when teller absent
- Counted daily
- Discrepancies reported immediately

**Large Deposits/Withdrawals:**
- >£1,000: Manager approval
- >£5,000: CTR form filed
- >£10,000: Internal controls check

---

### Customer Data Security

**Personal Information:**
- Never discuss on phone where customers can hear
- Don't leave documents on desk
- File customer documents in locked cabinet
- Shred documents when no longer needed

**System Access:**
- Log out when leaving desk
- Never share passwords
- Report suspicious activity immediately
- Lock computer when absent

---

### Fraud Detection

**Red Flags:**
- Customer unusually nervous/evasive
- Requesting large unusual transaction
- Using multiple accounts for same purpose
- Inconsistent personal information
- Third party insisting customer transfer funds

**Action:** Politely ask questions, escalate to manager/compliance if concerned

## PART 6: Accessibility & Inclusivity

### Physical Accessibility
- [ ] Wheelchair access: Ramps, accessible toilets
- [ ] Hearing loop: Available at teller counter
- [ ] Large print: Forms, information available
- [ ] Quiet area: Private space for sensitive discussions

### Communication
- Speak clearly and face customer
- Allow extra time for older/vulnerable customers
- Use plain language (no jargon)
- Offer written information
- Arrange interpreter if needed

### Vulnerable Customers
- Elderly or mentally incapacitated: Slow down, be patient
- Language barriers: Arrange interpreter
- Abuse victims: Offer safe space, confidential help
- Financial abuse: Escalate to compliance

## PART 8: Performance Standards

**Customer Service:**
- Greeting time: <2 minutes
- Resolution time: <15 minutes per transaction
- Customer satisfaction: >90%
- Queue time: <10 minutes average

**Accuracy:**
- Transaction accuracy: >99.9%
- Cash drawer: Zero discrepancies
- KYC completion: 100%
- AML screening: 100%

**Security:**
- Fraud/security incidents: Reported <24 hours
- Customer data breaches: Reported immediately
- Vault discrepancies: Zero tolerance
- Suspicious transactions: 100% reported
