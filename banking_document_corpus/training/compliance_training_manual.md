# MODULE 1: AML & SANCTIONS COMPLIANCE

## What Is AML?

**AML = Anti-Money Laundering**

Money laundering is the process of converting illegally obtained money into legitimate-looking funds. AML rules are designed to prevent this.

**Why It Matters:**
- FirstUK Bank must comply with UK law (Money Laundering Regulations 2017)
- Violations result in: FCA fines (>£10M), criminal prosecution, loss of license
- Your role: Identify and report suspicious activity

## The AML Process

```
Suspicious Activity Detected
    ↓
Report to Compliance Team
    ↓
Investigation
    ↓
Report to FCA (if necessary)
    ↓
Law Enforcement Action
```

## Red Flags: What to Look For

### Transaction Patterns

**High-Risk Patterns:**
- Multiple large deposits followed by immediate large withdrawals
- Frequent transfers to multiple accounts/jurisdictions
- Round-number transactions (£10k, £20k exactly)
- Transfers to high-risk countries
- Transactions inconsistent with customer profile

**Example:** A pensioner suddenly transferring £50,000 to a shell company in Panama

### Customer Behavior

**High-Risk Behavior:**
- Reluctance to provide documentation
- Vague about purpose of account
- Changes story about transaction purpose
- Uses third party as proxy
- Avoids using account normally after opening

**Example:** Customer opens account, deposits £30,000, then has friend withdraw it

### Business Activities

**High-Risk Businesses:**
- Money services (remittance)
- Casinos, gambling
- Art dealers, jewelers
- Cash-intensive retail
- Used car dealerships
- High-value goods (watches, handbags)

### Geographic Risk

**High-Risk Countries:**
- OFAC List: Iran, North Korea, Syria, Crimea, Cuba, Sudan
- EU Consolidated List: Various sanctioned entities
- HMT Sanctions: Additional UK-sanctioned entities
- High-corruption countries: Nigeria, Zimbabwe, Venezuela

## Screening Requirements

### New Customers

**Upon Account Opening:**
1. Obtain customer name, DOB, address
2. Screen against: OFAC, EU Consolidated List, HMT Sanctions, UN Sanctions, PEP Database
3. Review results (must be clear before account opens)
4. If match found: Escalate to Compliance immediately
5. Document screening result

### Ongoing Monitoring

**Monthly:**
- Review transactions >£5,000
- Check for unusual patterns
- Screen against updated sanctions lists
- Assess continuing customer risk

**If Match Found:**
- Don't process transaction
- Escalate to Compliance
- Do NOT inform customer (tipping-off is illegal)

## Sanctions List Abbreviations

| List | What It Covers |
|---|---|
| **OFAC** | US-sanctioned parties (Iran, North Korea, Syria, etc.) |
| **EU Consolidated** | EU-sanctioned individuals and entities |
| **HMT** | UK Home Treasury-sanctioned parties |
| **UN Sanctions** | UN-sanctioned individuals and entities |
| **PEP Database** | Politically Exposed Persons (high-risk individuals) |
| **Adverse Media** | Individuals in news for financial crime |

## Common Mistakes (Don't Make These!)

❌ **Mistake 1:** Ignoring a potential match and processing transaction anyway  
✅ **Correct:** Escalate to Compliance, do not process

❌ **Mistake 2:** Telling customer "We're checking if you're on a sanctions list"  
✅ **Correct:** Do not disclose screening to customer (tipping-off is illegal)

❌ **Mistake 3:** Closing account without investigation  
✅ **Correct:** Report suspicious activity to Compliance first

❌ **Mistake 4:** Only screening at account opening  
✅ **Correct:** Screen continuously throughout customer relationship

# MODULE 2: KNOW YOUR CUSTOMER (KYC)

## What Is KYC?

**KYC = Know Your Customer**

KYC rules require banks to:
1. Collect customer identity information
2. Verify customer identity
3. Understand customer's business/source of funds
4. Assess customer's risk level

## Documents Required

### Primary Identity Document (Choose One)

✓ **UK Passport**
- Original must be valid
- Copy both sides
- Check for tampering

✓ **UK Driving License**
- Original must be valid
- Copy both sides
- Check photo matches customer

❌ **Not Acceptable:**
- Expired documents
- Photocopies only (must see original)
- Documents from other countries

### Proof of Address (Choose One)

✓ **Utility Bill**
- Must be dated within 3 months
- Must show customer name and address
- Copy and file

✓ **Council Tax Letter**
- Must be current year
- Must show customer name and address
- Copy and file

✓ **Bank Statement**
- Must be dated within 3 months
- Must show customer name and address
- Copy and file

❌ **Not Acceptable:**
- Expired documents
- P45/P60 tax documents
- Tenancy agreements
- Mobile phone bills

## Verification Checklist

For **Every New Customer**, verify:

- [ ] Photo ID obtained and genuine
- [ ] Customer face matches ID photo
- [ ] ID document is valid (not expired)
- [ ] Proof of address dated within 3 months
- [ ] Customer name matches documents
- [ ] Address matches documents
- [ ] Copies securely filed
- [ ] System record complete
- [ ] No sanctions list matches

**If Any Check Fails:** Do not open account. Escalate to manager.

## Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

**For Higher-Risk Customers:**
- High-net-worth individuals (>£500k)
- PEPs (Politically Exposed Persons)
- Businesses in high-risk industries
- Customers in high-risk countries

**Additional Steps:**
- Verify source of funds
- Obtain business documents
- Understand purpose of account
- Ongoing monitoring
- Regular review

## Common Mistakes (Don't Make These!)

❌ **Mistake 1:** Opening account without seeing original ID  
✅ **Correct:** Always verify original, then copy

❌ **Mistake 2:** Accepting address proof dated >3 months ago  
✅ **Correct:** Require current document within 3 months

❌ **Mistake 3:** Relying on verbal description instead of documentation  
✅ **Correct:** Require documentation for every verification

❌ **Mistake 4:** Skipping KYC because customer is "nice"  
✅ **Correct:** KYC applies to EVERY customer, no exceptions

# MODULE 3: GDPR & DATA PROTECTION

## What Is GDPR?

**GDPR = General Data Protection Regulation**

EU/UK data protection law that regulates how companies collect, use, and store personal data.

## Key Principles

| Principle | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Lawfulness** | Data processing must be legal and justified |
| **Fairness** | Must be transparent with customers |
| **Purpose Limitation** | Only use data for stated purpose |
| **Data Minimization** | Only collect what's needed |
| **Accuracy** | Keep data current and correct |
| **Storage Limitation** | Only keep data as long as necessary |
| **Integrity & Confidentiality** | Protect data with security |
| **Accountability** | Document what you do with data |

## Customer Data Rights

### Right to Access
**Customer Request:** "I want to see all data you have on me"  
**Your Action:** 
- Acknowledge request
- Send to Data Protection Officer (dpo@firstuk.bank)
- DPO responds within 30 days with all data

### Right to Erasure
**Customer Request:** "Delete my data"  
**Your Action:**
- Acknowledge request
- Send to DPO
- DPO reviews legal requirements (may need to retain for regulatory reasons)
- Delete if legally possible

### Right to Portability
**Customer Request:** "Give me my data in a portable format"  
**Your Action:**
- Acknowledge request
- Send to DPO
- DPO provides structured, commonly used format (PDF, CSV, etc.)

### Right to Object
**Customer Request:** "Don't use my data for marketing"  
**Your Action:**
- Acknowledge request
- Stop marketing communications
- Document opt-out in system
- Respect going forward

### Right to Restrict Processing
**Customer Request:** "Don't process my data while you investigate"  
**Your Action:**
- Acknowledge request
- Pause processing (except legal requirements)
- Resume when investigation complete

## What Data Can We Collect?

### Necessary for Banking
✓ Name, DOB, address (identity verification)  
✓ Account information (service provision)  
✓ Transaction history (AML compliance)  
✓ Payment details (transaction processing)  

### Optional
? Contact preference (email/phone/SMS)  
? Marketing consent (separate opt-in)  
? Product preferences (service improvement)  

### Never Collect Without Consent
❌ Political beliefs  
❌ Religious beliefs  
❌ Sexual orientation  
❌ Health information (unless relevant)  
❌ Criminal records (unless legally required)  

## Data Security

**How We Protect Customer Data:**
- Encryption: TLS 1.3 (in transit), AES-256 (at rest)
- Access control: RBAC (role-based access)
- Audit logs: All access recorded
- Physical security: Locked cabinets, secure facilities
- Employee training: All staff trained on GDPR

**Your Responsibility:**
- Don't share passwords
- Log out when leaving desk
- Don't discuss customer data publicly
- Report security concerns immediately
- Lock documents when not in use

## Data Breach Response

**If You Discover a Breach:**
1. Stop further access
2. Report to manager immediately
3. Report to DPO (dpo@firstuk.bank)
4. Do NOT attempt to cover it up
5. DPO will notify FCA and customers within 72 hours

**Consequences of Breach:**
- Customer notification required
- FCA notification required
- Reputational damage
- Potential fines (up to €20M or 4% revenue)
- Criminal prosecution possible

## Common Mistakes (Don't Make These!)

❌ **Mistake 1:** Ignoring customer data access request  
✅ **Correct:** Forward to DPO within 24 hours

❌ **Mistake 2:** Sharing customer data with third party without consent  
✅ **Correct:** Only share with explicit permission or legal requirement

❌ **Mistake 3:** Keeping customer data longer than necessary  
✅ **Correct:** Delete/archive when retention period expires

❌ **Mistake 4:** Discussing customer data in public (overhearing customers)  
✅ **Correct:** Have sensitive conversations in private area

# MODULE 4: FRAUD PREVENTION

## What Is Fraud?

Fraud is deception for financial gain. At FirstUK Bank, we combat fraud through:
- Detection systems
- Staff training
- Customer education
- Rapid response

## Types of Fraud

### Account Takeover
**What It Is:** Fraudster gains access to customer's account  
**Signs:** Unusual transactions, login from new device, password change  
**Prevention:** 2FA, strong passwords, monitoring

### Impersonation
**What It Is:** Fraudster pretends to be customer or FirstUK staff  
**Signs:** Customer claims "I didn't authorize this"  
**Prevention:** Verify identity, document authorization

### Unauthorized Transactions
**What It Is:** Fraudster uses customer's card/account without permission  
**Signs:** Large transaction, unusual merchant, foreign location  
**Prevention:** Fraud detection, customer education, monitoring

### Insider Fraud
**What It Is:** Staff member uses position to commit fraud  
**Signs:** Unusual account access, undocumented transactions, authority abuse  
**Prevention:** Access controls, audit logs, whistleblower reporting

## Red Flags During Customer Interaction

**Be Alert If Customer:**
- Seems coached or nervous
- Can't explain purpose of transaction
- Uses unusual channels or amounts
- Third party is present and controlling conversation
- Requests cash immediately after deposit

**Be Alert If Teller Colleague:**
- Bypasses controls
- Works unusual hours alone
- Has access they shouldn't have
- Makes undocumented exceptions

## Your Role in Fraud Prevention

### Recognize Fraud
- Monitor for suspicious transactions
- Ask questions if something seems off
- Trust your instincts
- Report concerns

### Escalate Fraud
- Don't confront fraudster
- Document observations
- Report to manager
- Report to Compliance/Financial Crime team
- Follow [[policy_fraud]] procedures

### Prevent Fraud
- Follow KYC/AML procedures
- Use secure passwords
- Lock your computer
- Don't share credentials
- Monitor your own account

### Help Customers Prevent Fraud
- Educate on security (strong passwords, 2FA)
- Encourage transaction monitoring
- Advise on phishing scams
- Promote card security practices

## Customer Fraud Protection

### Direct Debit Guarantee
**What It Covers:**
- Unauthorized Direct Debits: Full refund within 10 days
- Wrong amount charged: Refund within 10 days
- Duplicate payments: Refund within 10 days

**Customer Rights:**
- No questions asked refund
- No liability
- Automatic protection

### Chargeback (Card Fraud)
**What It Covers:**
- Unauthorized card transactions: Dispute with card scheme
- Merchant error: Dispute with card scheme
- Item not received: Dispute with merchant/scheme

**Timeline:**
- Report fraud within 120 days
- Bank investigates and disputes with merchant
- Refund if upheld (typically 3–6 months)

### Our Liability
FirstUK Bank is liable for:
- Unauthorized transactions you report within 120 days
- If you followed reasonable security measures
- Up to account balance

FirstUK Bank is NOT liable for:
- Transactions you authorized (even if regretted)
- Gross negligence (shared password, written PIN)
- Fraud you failed to report for >120 days

## Common Mistakes (Don't Make These!)

❌ **Mistake 1:** Assuming fraudster is obvious  
✅ **Correct:** Fraudsters can be very convincing; stay alert

❌ **Mistake 2:** Not reporting suspicious activity  
✅ **Correct:** Report ALL concerns to Compliance

❌ **Mistake 3:** Ignoring customer fraud concerns  
✅ **Correct:** Take all fraud reports seriously

❌ **Mistake 4:** Sharing access credentials  
✅ **Correct:** Keep credentials private always

# MODULE 5: REPORTING OBLIGATIONS

## Types of Reports

### Suspicious Activity Report (SAR)

**When to File:**
- Suspicious transaction >£5,000
- Pattern of unusual activity
- Potential money laundering
- Fraud indicators
- Sanctions list match

**How to File:**
1. Document suspicious activity
2. Report to Compliance Manager
3. Complete SAR form
4. Submit to FCA (within 30 days)
5. Document filing

**Example:** Customer deposits £30k cash, withdraws £30k to unknown party next day → File SAR

### Currency Transaction Report (CTR)

**When to File:**
- Any transaction >£10,000 in cash
- Within 5 business days
- To FCA

**What to Report:**
- Customer name and ID
- Transaction date and amount
- Transaction purpose (if known)
- Whether customer is new or existing

**Example:** Customer withdraws £15,000 in cash → File CTR

### Counterparty Transaction Report (CCTR)

**When to File:**
- Transaction involving high-risk countries
- Transaction with PEP or sanctioned party
- Wire transfer outside UK

**What to Report:**
- Customer details
- Counterparty details
- Transaction details
- Risk assessment

### Whistleblower Reports

**When to Use:**
- You suspect fraud by colleague
- You observe policy violations
- You see compliance breaches
- You witness misconduct

**How to Report:**
- Internal: Speak to manager or HR
- Confidential: Speak to Data Protection Officer (dpo@firstuk.bank)
- External: Contact FCA Whistleblowing (0300 500 0023)

**Protection:**
- Whistleblowers are protected by law
- No retaliation permitted
- Confidentiality maintained
- Investigation conducted

## Documentation & Record Keeping

**What to Document:**
- Date, time, location
- People involved
- Details of activity
- Why you suspect violation
- Actions taken
- Outcome

**How Long to Keep:**
- Customer files: 6 years after closure
- Transaction records: 6 years
- KYC/AML: 5–6 years
- Suspicious activity reports: 5 years minimum

**Storage:**
- Secure filing (locked cabinet)
- Digital records encrypted
- Access limited to authorized staff
- Backed up regularly

# MODULE 6: CONSEQUENCES OF NON-COMPLIANCE

## What Happens If You Don't Comply?

### To FirstUK Bank

**FCA Fines:**
- AML violations: Up to £10M+ per incident
- GDPR breaches: Up to €20M or 4% revenue
- Customer protection failures: Up to £50M+
- Recent example: HSBC fined £1.9B for AML failures

**Regulatory Action:**
- Enforcement investigation
- Asset freezes
- License suspension
- Loss of banking license (extreme case)

**Reputational Damage:**
- Media coverage
- Customer loss
- Investor confidence drop
- Business disruption

### To You (Staff Member)

**Criminal Liability:**
- Money laundering facilitation: Up to 14 years prison
- Fraud: Up to 10 years prison
- Data protection breach: Up to 2 years prison
- Tipping-off (warning customer of screening): Criminal offense

**Professional Consequences:**
- Dismissal from FirstUK Bank
- Industry ban (you can't work in banking)
- Professional certification revoked
- Difficulty finding employment

**Financial Consequences:**
- Criminal fines
- Civil penalties
- Compensation to victims
- Legal costs

### Real Examples

**Case 1: Danske Bank (AML Failure)**
- Issue: Allowed £200B suspicious money through
- Consequence: €1B fine, CEO resigned, reputation destroyed
- Why: Staff ignored suspicious transactions

**Case 2: Wells Fargo (Account Fraud)**
- Issue: Staff created fake accounts without authorization
- Consequence: $3B fine, CEO prosecution, massive layoffs
- Why: Pressure to meet targets; ignored ethics

**Case 3: Facebook (GDPR Violation)**
- Issue: Shared user data without consent
- Consequence: €110M fine, ongoing investigation
- Why: Didn't respect user data rights

## How to Protect Yourself

✓ **Complete Training:** Finish all modules and assessments  
✓ **Ask Questions:** If unsure, escalate to manager/compliance  
✓ **Follow Procedures:** Use the SOPs and policies [[policy_aml]], [[policy_customer_identification]], [[policy_privacy]], etc.  
✓ **Report Issues:** Don't ignore suspicious activity  
✓ **Document Actions:** Keep records of what you did  
✓ **Stay Updated:** Attend refresher training annually  
✓ **Maintain Ethics:** Your integrity matters
