## Q1 2026 Compliance Bulletins

### Bulletin #1: Enhanced KYC Requirements Effective March 1, 2026

**Date Issued:** 2026-01-20  
**Effective:** 2026-03-01  
**Audience:** All Customer-Facing Staff, Compliance  

**Summary:**
FCA has issued enhanced guidance on Know Your Customer (KYC) procedures for high-risk customers. All staff must be aware of new requirements before March 1, 2026.

**Key Changes:**
1. **Beneficial Ownership:** If customer is a company/trust, we must verify actual owners (>25% ownership)
2. **Source of Funds:** Large deposits (>£10,000) require explanation of where money came from
3. **Enhanced Monitoring:** High-risk customers (PEP, foreign, high-value) reviewed quarterly not annually

**Your Responsibility:**
- If opening account, collect source of funds info for large deposits
- If customer is company, ask about real owners
- Flag any unclear ownership structures to Compliance
- Ask customer where money is coming from (friendly, non-suspicious tone)

**Example Scenarios:**
- Customer opens account with £50,000 deposit
  - Ask: "Where is this deposit coming from?" (salary, savings, business, inheritance?)
  - Verify: Request recent payslip, savings statement, or business accounts
  
- Customer is limited company
  - Ask: "Who are the beneficial owners of this company?"
  - Verify: Request shareholders list, director information

**Training:** Mandatory training session Friday, February 15, 2026 (90 minutes)  
**Questions:** Email compliance@firstuk.bank

### Bulletin #2: GDPR SAR Updates – New Timelines

**Date Issued:** 2026-01-25  
**Effective:** Immediate  
**Audience:** All Staff (especially Operations)  

**Summary:**
ICO (UK data protection regulator) clarified Subject Access Request (SAR) handling procedures. We must respond to SARs within 30 days, not 60. No extensions allowed unless request is complex.

**Key Changes:**
1. **Response Deadline:** 30 days (not 60) from request date
2. **Extensions:** Only if request is complex; must explain why to customer
3. **Format:** Must provide data in electronic format (CSV, PDF, etc.)
4. **Fees:** Cannot charge unless request "clearly unfounded"

**Your Responsibility:**
- If customer requests their personal data, log immediately
- Assign to Operations Manager
- Operations Manager: Compile data within 30 days
- Get Compliance approval before sending to customer

**Timeline:**
- Day 1: Customer requests SAR (email, phone, or form)
- Day 2: Log in system + send receipt to customer
- Day 25: Send draft to Compliance for review
- Day 30: Send to customer (latest)

**Questions:** Email dpo@firstuk.bank

### Bulletin #3: Account Opening Errors – Reduce by 50%

**Date Issued:** 2026-02-01  
**Effective:** Immediate  
**Audience:** Branch Managers, Customer Service  

**Summary:**
Recent audit found 15 account opening errors in January (0.5% error rate). Target: <0.25% by end of Q1. Key errors: wrong interest rate applied, missing KYC documentation, incorrect address.

**Common Errors & How to Prevent:**
1. **Interest Rate Error** — Double-check Pricing Guide (PRC-GUIDE-2026) for current rates
2. **KYC Missing** — Use account opening checklist (confirm ID + address before processing)
3. **Address Mismatch** — Ensure ID address matches application form

**Your Responsibility:**
- Review account opening checklist before submitting application
- Have manager sign-off on all applications >£10,000 opening deposit
- Contact customer if any information unclear (don't assume)

**Quality Check:**
- Before submission: Account Manager reviews for errors
- Daily: Operations Manager spot-checks 5 applications
- Weekly: Customer Service Manager audits sample files

**Zero Tolerance:** Repeated errors = retraining required

### Bulletin #4: Phishing Alert – Real Attempt in Branch

**Date Issued:** 2026-02-10  
**Effective:** Immediate  
**Audience:** All Staff  

**Summary:**
On February 8, 2026, a customer arrived at London Canary Wharf branch claiming to be "security team" requesting customer account access to "verify fraud." Suspected social engineering attack. Staff correctly declined. Alert: Fraudsters may approach branches pretending to be bank staff.

**Red Flags to Watch For:**
- Visitor claims to be from bank wanting account access
- Caller claims to be from Head Office requesting staff credentials
- Email from "senior management" asking for customer list
- Customer reports suspicious phone call claiming to verify transactions

**What to Do:**
- Verify identity by calling back using known number (not number provided)
- Never share customer data without proper authorization
- Never share staff login credentials
- Report suspicious activity immediately to manager

**Report To:**
- Security Alert email: security@firstuk.bank
- If urgent: Manager immediately + 0800-555-SECURITY
