## Executive Summary

FirstUK Bank adopts a **conservative risk appetite** balancing customer service delivery, business growth, and regulatory compliance. The bank will not pursue growth at the expense of financial stability, customer protection, or regulatory compliance.

---

## Risk Appetite Philosophy

**We seek to achieve sustainable profitability through:**
1. **High-Quality Customer Service** — Reliable, fair banking services
2. **Financial Stability** — Capital above regulatory minimums with buffers
3. **Strong Compliance** — Zero tolerance for willful regulatory breaches
4. **Operational Excellence** — Reliable systems, robust controls, continuous improvement

**We will NOT pursue:**
- High-risk lending strategies
- Aggressive growth in loss-making areas
- Shortcuts around compliance or controls
- Concentrated exposure to single customers/sectors
- Excessive leverage or capital risk

## Risk Appetite by Category

### 1. Credit Risk

**Appetite:** Conservative

**Tolerance:**
- **Loan Loss Ratio:** <0.5% annually
- **Delinquency Rate:** <2% of active accounts
- **Concentration:** No single customer >10% of capital
- **Mortgage LTV:** <80% (maximum)

**Rationale:**
FirstUK Bank focuses on consumer lending (mortgages) with strong underwriting. Does not pursue commercial lending or risky credit products.

**Controls:**
- Credit scoring mandatory for mortgages
- Affordability assessment per FCA rules
- Arrears management and escalation procedures
- Regular loan quality reviews

---

### 2. Operational Risk

**Appetite:** Low

**Tolerance:**
- **Annual Operational Losses:** <£50,000
- **Major Incidents:** <1 per year
- **System Outages:** <8 hours per year
- **Process Errors:** <0.5% error rate

**Rationale:**
FirstUK Bank prioritizes operational reliability, customer trust, and regulatory compliance. Invests in systems, training, and controls to prevent operational failures.

**Controls:**
- Comprehensive SOPs and procedures
- Staff training and competency assessment
- System redundancy and disaster recovery
- Automated controls where possible
- Regular audits and monitoring

---

### 3. Compliance & Regulatory Risk

**Appetite:** Minimal/Zero

**Tolerance:**
- **Regulatory Breaches:** Zero willful violations
- **KYC/AML Non-Compliance:** 0% tolerance
- **Customer Protection Breaches:** 0% tolerance
- **Disclosure Violations:** Zero

**Rationale:**
FirstUK Bank operates under close regulatory oversight. Any regulatory breach damages reputation and license. Invests heavily in compliance infrastructure.

**Controls:**
- Compliance policies and procedures (see Policies)
- Regular regulatory scanning and updates
- Staff training (mandatory annual)
- Audit testing of compliance controls
- Regulatory relationship management

---

### 4. Fraud Risk

**Appetite:** Low

**Tolerance:**
- **Annual Fraud Loss:** <£100,000 (0.3% of revenue)
- **Fraud Detection Rate:** >96%
- **Customer Reimbursement:** <£30k annually
- **Internal Fraud:** Zero tolerance

**Rationale:**
FirstUK Bank invests in fraud prevention systems and monitoring. Committed to protecting customers and bank assets from fraud.

**Controls:**
- Advanced fraud detection engine (real-time)
- Transaction monitoring and rules
- Customer education and awareness
- Investigation procedures
- Segregation of duties

---

### 5. Reputational Risk

**Appetite:** Low

**Tolerance:**
- **Major Complaints:** <2 per 1,000 accounts
- **Regulatory Complaints:** <1 per 1,000 accounts
- **Media Incidents:** Zero negative incidents (aspirational)
- **Customer Trust:** NPS >70 (Net Promoter Score)

**Rationale:**
FirstUK Bank's brand depends on customer trust and operational reliability. Manages reputation actively through customer service, compliance, and transparency.

**Controls:**
- Customer service excellence (NPS tracking)
- Fast complaint resolution
- Transparent communication with regulators
- Crisis communication planning
- Social media monitoring

---

### 6. Capital Risk

**Appetite:** Conservative

**Tolerance:**
- **Capital Ratio (Pillar 1):** >15% minimum, 18%+ target
- **Leverage Ratio:** >5% (per Basel III)
- **Dividend Policy:** Conservative (retain earnings for growth)
- **Capital Adequacy Buffer:** 3%+ above minimum

**Rationale:**
FirstUK Bank maintains strong capital ratios to absorb losses, fund growth, and meet regulatory requirements. Does not maximize shareholder returns at expense of stability.

**Controls:**
- Annual capital planning
- Stress testing (multiple scenarios)
- Capital forecasting
- Dividend policy (Board-approved)
- Regular ratio monitoring

---

### 7. Technology & Cybersecurity Risk

**Appetite:** Low

**Tolerance:**
- **System Availability:** 99.8% (SLA)
- **Major Data Breach:** Zero tolerance
- **Cybersecurity Incidents:** <1 per year
- **Unplanned Downtime:** <2 hours per year

**Rationale:**
FirstUK Bank depends on reliable technology. Invests in security, resilience, and disaster recovery to maintain service availability and protect customer data.

**Controls:**
- Regular security assessments
- Penetration testing (annual)
- Incident response planning
- Disaster recovery procedures
- Staff security training

---

### 8. Liquidity Risk

**Appetite:** Conservative

**Tolerance:**
- **Loan-to-Deposit Ratio:** <90%
- **Liquid Assets Buffer:** 30 days+ operating expenses
- **Maturity Mismatch:** <6 months average

**Rationale:**
FirstUK Bank maintains strong liquidity to meet customer withdrawals and operational needs. Does not rely on external funding.

**Controls:**
- Deposit mix management
- Liquidity forecasting
- Asset-liability management
- Borrowing facilities (backup)
- Stress testing (liquidity scenarios)

## Risk Appetite Statement in Practice

### Risk Decisions

**Decisions Consistent with Risk Appetite:**
- ✅ Account opening with strong KYC, quality screening
- ✅ Mortgage lending with affordability checks, reasonable LTV
- ✅ Conservative pricing (competitive, not predatory)
- ✅ Investment in compliance and fraud systems
- ✅ Capital retention for stability and growth

**Decisions Inconsistent with Risk Appetite:**
- ❌ High-risk lending (commercial loans, high LTV mortgages)
- ❌ Aggressive growth (sacrificing control/compliance)
- ❌ Regulatory shortcuts or violations
- ❌ Concentrated customer exposure (>10% capital)
- ❌ Excessive leverage or capital risk
- ❌ Weak controls or inadequate staffing

### Escalation & Exception Handling

**If Proposed Decision Conflicts with Risk Appetite:**
1. Risk team flags the issue
2. Business unit provides rationale (why necessary)
3. Executive committee reviews (CRO, CFO, CEO)
4. Board Risk Committee notified (if significant)
5. Documented decision and justification recorded

## Risk Monitoring & Reporting

### Key Risk Indicators (KRIs) Monitored

| KRI | Frequency | Threshold | Action if Exceeded |
|-----|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Loan loss ratio | Quarterly | >0.5% | Review underwriting criteria |
| Operational incidents | Monthly | >4/month | Investigate root causes |
| Compliance violations | Monthly | >0 | Immediate remediation |
| Fraud loss | Quarterly | >£25k/q | Enhance detection rules |
| System availability | Daily | <99.8% | Emergency response |
| Capital ratio | Daily | <15% | Escalate to Board |
| Customer complaints | Monthly | >2/1000 | Process review |

### Reporting Frequency

- **Daily:** Capital ratio, system availability, fraud incidents
- **Weekly:** Operational incidents, compliance alerts
- **Monthly:** KRI summary to management, operational losses
- **Quarterly:** Full risk dashboard to Board Risk Committee
- **Annually:** Comprehensive risk assessment to Board
